    Why this ledger remains one page

    This page is an auditable research register rather than a sequential instructional volume. Keeping the admission criteria, evidence status, and family records together makes cross-entry review and maintenance explicit.

    This ledger records the primary-source research audit behind the family curriculum. It is not a leaderboard. Its purpose is to distinguish mature foundations from protocol- or assumption-specific research and prevent publication recency, workshop results, or attractive analogies from silently becoming established consensus.

    Evidence Labels¤

    • Established: a result with mature theory or repeated use whose assumptions are well understood.
    • Peer-reviewed research: an archival conference/journal result that remains protocol- or assumption-specific.
    • Preprint: publicly available research without confirmed archival peer review for the cited version.
    • Technical report: an organization or author report; useful but not equivalent to independent review.
    • Vendor claim: a product/model claim whose data, code, or protocol may be incomplete.
    • Interpretive synthesis: a curriculum-level connection derived from several sources rather than stated as a theorem by one paper.

    Dates below identify the cited version or venue; the page metadata records when statuses were verified. Submissions, withdrawn or rejected papers, and workshop versions are not promoted to peer-reviewed research evidence.

    Probabilistic Circuits¤

    Probabilistic Circuits: What is established¤

    Smooth and decomposable circuits support tractable marginal evaluation under tractable leaves. Determinism and structured decomposability enable additional queries and compatible circuit operations. These are property-specific guarantees, not a universal statement that every PC query is linear time. The canonical source is Choi, Vergari, and Van den Broeck's framework.

    Probabilistic Circuits: Research conclusion¤

    Probabilistic-circuit research studies the expressivity–query–size–optimisation trade-off through compatible structures, adaptive minibatch learning, hybrid leaves, relational objects, and exact expectations or conditionals. Curriculum comparisons should therefore evaluate query workload, circuit size, and optimisation protocol—not only sample metrics or whether a circuit replaces diffusion on unconstrained raw generation.

    Implicit Generators Beyond GANs¤

    Implicit Generators Beyond GANs: What is established¤

    An implicit model is defined by sampling without requiring pointwise density. MMD with characteristic kernels identifies distributions at population scale; IMLE assigns generated neighbors to data examples; sliced Wasserstein compares one-dimensional projections. None of these statements guarantees good finite-batch gradients or modern perceptual quality.

    Implicit Generators Beyond GANs: Research conclusion¤

    Standalone GMMN/IMLE/sliced-Wasserstein generator research is less active than diffusion and flow research. The ideas remain relevant inside few-shot synthesis, learned codecs, imitation policies, simulator inference, and evaluation. The curriculum preserves them as distinct objective families without manufacturing a false state-of-the-art narrative.

    Discrete Iterative Generation¤

    Discrete Iterative Generation: What is established¤

    D3PMs define discrete Markov corruption and a variational objective. CTMC reverse rates require density ratios. Masked iterative samplers depend materially on unmasking/remasking policy. Parallel token updates trade sequential depth for within-step conditional independence.

    Discrete Iterative Generation: Research conclusion¤

    Discrete iterative research covers objective simplification, revisable corruption, block/semi-autoregressive hybrids, sampler theory, architecture specialization, and post-training in addition to basic generative capability. Peer-reviewed theory sharpens a central lesson: parallelism is controlled by dependency/information structure and schedule knowledge, not merely by choosing to unmask more tokens. Every speed claim must include sequence length, block size, number of changing tokens, NFE, and wall clock.

    Bayesian Flow Networks¤

    What Is Established Versus Active Research¤

    Conjugate Gaussian and categorical Bayesian updates are established probability. The claim that a full BFN provides a competitive generative framework has a different evidence status. The original BFN paper is a preprint/technical research report; treat its framework as canonical to the family but its benchmark conclusions as preprint evidence.

    Bayesian Flow Networks: Research conclusion¤

    BFNs deserve a standalone guide because the distribution-parameter state and Bayesian message semantics are genuinely distinct pedagogical objects. The evidence base is still smaller than diffusion's. Curriculum language must say “promising/derived correspondence,” not “replacement for diffusion.”

    Generative Flow Networks¤

    Generative Flow Networks: What is established¤

    Under exact balance on the specified constructive graph, terminal probability is proportional to reward. Flow matching, detailed balance, trajectory balance, and subtrajectory balance impose conservation at different scales. Practical convergence still depends on exploration and function approximation.

    Generative Flow Networks: Research conclusion¤

    GFlowNet research spans objective design, exploration, partial-episode policy evaluation, richer constructive DAGs, support-aware offline training, continuous/diffusion extensions, reward conditioning, pretraining, and prospective scientific validation. Peer-reviewed evidence shows that unvisited high-reward regions, state-graph design, length effects, and incomplete logged support remain bottlenecks even when sampled balance equations are small. Best-reward tables are insufficient; proportionality, coverage, oracle cost, and amortization cost are the defining evidence.

    Point Processes and Random Finite Sets¤

    Point Processes and Random Finite Sets: What is established¤

    Point processes define laws on locally finite counting measures; finite simple configurations can also be represented as random finite sets. Conditional intensity determines temporal likelihood only together with its compensator and observation window. Janossy or set densities require an explicit reference measure and factorial convention. Poisson, renewal, Hawkes, self-correcting, Cox, Gibbs, and determinantal models make different independence or interaction assumptions, while inversion, thinning, branching, spectral, and MCMC algorithms are samplers rather than alternative definitions of the generated object.

    Work Evidence Contribution used in the curriculum Boundary
    Hawkes self-exciting processes, 1971 Established foundation Linear history-dependent excitation and kernel-based dependence Stationarity and branching claims require nonnegative linear kernels and the stated spectral condition
    Self-correcting point process, 1979 Established foundation Event-triggered inhibition and count regulation Not a generic model of every repulsive spatial or temporal process

    Add and Thin, NeurIPS 2023 Peer-reviewed research Configuration-level addition and thinning transitions One learned iterative sampler, not the definition of point-process probability

    Reliable Detection of Empty Space, ICLR 2026 Peer-reviewed research Marked spatial point-process treatment of object detections and empty regions Detection protocols and marks bound the result; not a universal spatial generator comparison
    EarthquakeNPP, TMLR 2026 Peer-reviewed counterevidence Leakage-corrected neural/classical earthquake comparison Domain- and protocol-specific; supports strong baselines rather than a universal family ranking

    Point Processes and Random Finite Sets: Research conclusion¤

    The active design space includes flexible intensity and cumulative-hazard networks, normalized conditional flows, latent intensity fields, configuration transitions, score objectives, logical constraints, and amortized inference. Progress must be evaluated on the whole law: counts and empty windows, event times, spatial interaction, marks, censoring, residual dependence, long-horizon rollouts, and compute. A lower next-event error or a faster parallel sampler does not by itself establish a better point-process generator.

    Random Graphs and Relational Generators¤

    Random Graphs and Relational Generators: What is established¤

    The complete Random Graphs and Relational Generators volume develops the family and its evaluation contracts. A random-graph model places a law on relational structures, not merely on one convenient adjacency ordering. For arbitrary node labels, an unlabeled probability sums labeled masses over an isomorphism class; the automorphism shortcut additionally requires an invariant labeled law. Erdős–Rényi, stochastic block, latent-position, ERGM, graphon, and sparse exchangeable-process constructions encode different dependence and graph-size assumptions. An autoregressive order, equivariant network, latent code, energy, flow, denoising chain, circuit, or GFlowNet is a possible composition with those semantics rather than a substitute for them.

    Random Graphs and Relational Generators: Research conclusion¤

    Graph-generation research is broadening the probability path, representation, topology features, exact-query interface, constraint mechanism, and evaluation lens. No single trend removes the need to declare graph type, node-count law, labeling convention, data regime, target probability or reward, sampler cost, and scientific query. Whole-graph IID generation, one-network link prediction, molecular optimization, and posterior graph inference remain different experiments even when they reuse the same graph architecture.

    Copula Models¤

    Copula Models: What is established¤

    Sklar's theorem separates a multivariate CDF into declared marginals and a dependence distribution with uniform marginals. Whole-cube uniqueness requires continuous marginals; discrete observations identify only the values needed on their marginal range grid. Elliptical, Archimedean, extreme-value, and pair-copula constructions encode different symmetry, tail, and conditional-dependence assumptions. A neural model supported on the cube is not thereby a copula: marginal uniformity needs its own proof or construction.

    The complete derivations, worked examples, and evaluation protocol are in Copula Models.

    Table FRONT.8. What is established
    Work Evidence Contribution used in the curriculum Boundary

    Vines—A New Graphical Model, 2002 Established foundation Regular-vine organization of conditional pair copulas Factorization graph is not a causal graph; practical searches add assumptions
    The \(t\) Copula and Related Copulas, 2005 Established foundation Elliptical tail-dependence construction and formula Asymptotic tail dependence does not determine every finite-threshold risk
    A Primer on Copulas for Count Data, 2007 Established foundation Discrete nonuniqueness, ties, and cell-probability semantics Continuous density and rank formulas do not transfer unchanged
    Multivariate Archimedean Copulas, 2009 Established foundation \(d\)-monotone generator criterion and sampling representation A generic decreasing or neural generator is insufficient
    Implicit Generative Copulas, NeurIPS 2021 Peer-reviewed research Implicit dependence generation followed by marginal uniformization Does not provide every density or exact finite-sample calibration
    Generative Archimedean Copulas, UAI 2021 Peer-reviewed research Neural latent mixing distribution for valid Archimedean construction Inherits Archimedean and hierarchy restrictions
    Your Copula Is a Classifier in Disguise, AISTATS 2025 Peer-reviewed research Copula density as a joint-versus-product density ratio Population-optimal classifier identity does not guarantee normalized finite estimates or direct sampling
    Diffusion and Flow-based Copulas, ICLR 2026 Peer-reviewed research Dependence-forgetting processes that preserve marginal distributions Does not make arbitrary flow or diffusion processes valid copulas
    Probabilistic Rainfall Downscaling, JMLR 2026 Peer-reviewed research Censored latent Gaussian-copula composition with learned spatial marginals Application, observation model, kernel, and spatial regime bound transfer

    Copula Models: Research conclusion¤

    Copula research is expanding the ways dependence can be parameterized without removing the uniform-marginal constraint. Classifiers offer density ratios; implicit networks offer samples after uniformization; structured generators preserve known family validity; and marginal-preserving paths support new density and sampling procedures. These are different query interfaces. Their comparison must control the marginal estimator, tie and censoring treatment, density access, conditional queries, tail protocol, and sampling cost.

    Tensor-Network and Born Probability Models¤

    Tensor-Network and Born Probability Models: What is established¤

    A tensor-network probability model factorizes a high-dimensional table or amplitude into contracted cores. Nonnegative models normalize a nonnegative contraction; Born models normalize its squared magnitude. Matrix product states make normalization, marginals, conditionals, and sequential sampling polynomial in sequence length and bond rank under their chain structure, but general tensor-network contraction depends on topology and contraction order. A classically stored Born MPS uses quantum probability algebra without requiring quantum hardware.

    The complete derivations, finite examples, family relationships, and evaluation protocol are in Tensor-Network and Born Probability Models.

    Tensor-Network and Born Probability Models: Research conclusion¤

    Current work extends tensor probability models from discrete chains to continuous bases and circuit structures, and explores them as exact-query components inside larger generators. The durable boundary remains structural: rank and order control representation, topology and contraction plan control queries, and shared Born semantics do not establish a quantum computational advantage.

    Probabilistic Grammars and Program Derivations¤

    Probabilistic Grammars and Program Derivations: What is established¤

    A probabilistic grammar defines a law over finite recursive derivations and pushes that law through a yield or execution map. Ambiguous observations require summing over compatible derivations. Local normalization of rules does not by itself establish almost-sure finite termination. Inside–outside dynamic programming exactly marginalizes context-free trees under its grammar and cycle assumptions; neural parameterization changes the rule scores but not those semantics. Program generators add a second many-to-one map through execution, where types, exceptions, nontermination, and semantic equivalence become part of the probability and evaluation contract.

    The complete derivations, finite counterexamples, hybrid relationships, and evaluation protocol are in Probabilistic Grammars and Program Derivations.

    Probabilistic Grammars and Program Derivations: Research conclusion¤

    The active design space separates recursive probability semantics, neural parameterization, exact or approximate marginalization, executable program induction, and constrained sampling. Current work improves compact neural grammars, grammar induction, and formal-language support for left-to-right and arbitrary-order generation. None removes the need to prove finite termination, sum ambiguous derivations, distinguish local masking from global conditioning, and evaluate syntax, behavior, equivalence, and compute separately.

    Stochastic-Process and Function-Space Generators¤

    Stochastic-Process and Function-Space Generators: What is established¤

    Gaussian processes define projectively consistent finite-dimensional laws. Conditional Neural Processes amortize set-conditioned prediction, while latent variants introduce shared uncertainty. Neural SDEs and stochastic operators define path/field distributions only together with their driving noise, initial/boundary conditions, and discretization semantics.

    Stochastic-Process and Function-Space Generators: Research conclusion¤

    The decisive research question is not whether a network accepts arbitrary coordinates, but whether its predictions remain coherent across query sets, conditioning updates, resolutions, and observation patterns. The conditioning-consistency result sharpens the distinction between projective target consistency and learned conditioning coherence; the operator/sampler results broaden function-space generation without eliminating reference-measure or discretization assumptions. Function-space generators need projective, conditioning, pathwise, and refinement tests in addition to ordinary pointwise metrics.

    Causal Generative Models¤

    Causal Generative Models: What is established¤

    Structural causal models distinguish observational, interventional, and counterfactual distributions by specifying modular mechanisms and exogenous variables. Identification is prior to estimation: an expressive VAE, flow, GAN, diffusion model, or GFlowNet can parameterize a causal workflow, but observational fit cannot select among every interventionally distinct SCM.

    Causal Generative Models: Research conclusion¤

    Research is increasingly explicit about equivalence classes, intervention representations, hidden structure, selection, and distribution shift. Positive results for bijective mechanisms, grouping, or interventional coverage illustrate that counterfactual and representation identification is possible only after the extra structure is named. That is progress in specifying what can be recovered—not evidence that causal variables, graph orientation, or individual counterfactuals can be learned from arbitrary IID observations. Evaluation must separate sample realism, interventional prediction, structural recovery, population effects, and unit-level counterfactual validity.

    Cross-Family Research Conclusions¤

    1. Tractability moves rather than disappears. PCs pay with circuit structure/size; implicit generators pay with witness estimation; discrete iterative models pay with repeated transitions; BFNs pay with receiver approximation; GFlowNets pay with exploration and balance.
    2. Parallelism changes conditional assumptions. Masked models and BFNs update many coordinates per step but must recover dependencies across iterations.
    3. A unifying equation is not a benchmark equivalence. CTMC generators, SDE correspondences, and conservation identities clarify relationships without equalizing finite networks or solvers.
    4. Scientific-generation claims need external validation. Reward or validity metrics can be exploited; novelty and high predicted reward are not laboratory success.
    5. Maturity must remain visible. A 2026 accepted paper is peer-reviewed research, not established doctrine; an influential preprint remains a preprint.
