    5. Expressivity, Deep Circuits, and Hybrids¤

    Chapter 5 prerequisites and learning objectives¤

    This chapter assumes the query/property map and learning procedures from Chapters 2–4. By the end, you should be able to:

    1. distinguish universal representability from polynomial-size expressive efficiency;
    2. explain why determinism or a fixed vtree can cause exponential size gaps;
    3. audit which variable retains exact density in neural, flow, autoencoding, and distilled hybrids;
    4. classify representation-learning and graph-circuit results by evidence maturity; and
    5. design a hybrid whose retained queries are stated before its benchmark claims.

    The Expressivity–Tractability Trade-off¤

    Circuit size is the price of exact queries. A distribution may have a compact neural sampler but require a very large circuit under determinism or a fixed vtree. Conversely, a circuit can compactly reuse repeated context-specific independence that a flat mixture expands exponentially.

    Claims that one circuit class “subsumes” another refer to representational size under specified constraints, not automatically easier training or better finite-data generalization. Query guarantees must be carried through the translation.

    The size issue survives approximation. Peer-reviewed research: Leland and Choi, NeurIPS 2025 study approximation under \(f\)-divergences, prove hardness for broad tractable-marginal model classes, and establish an exponential approximation-size separation between decomposable circuits and deterministic decomposable circuits. This does not say practical deterministic PCs are always large; it shows that allowing small approximation error does not erase worst-case expressivity costs.

    Preprint research: Zhao et al., 2026 compare autoregressive PCs with Transformer language models. They identify probability-space convex-combination and fixed-routing bottlenecks, propose logit-space parameterization, and analyze vtree-aligned separation rank. As a May 2026 preprint, these results are an important hypothesis/theory checkpoint, not established evidence that one parameterization closes the language-modeling gap across scales.

    Neural Leaves and Conditional Circuits¤

    Leaves may be flexible tractable distributions whose parameters come from neural networks. Conditional PCs allow weights or leaf parameters to depend on context \(c\), producing \(p(x\mid c)\). For each fixed context, parameters must remain normalized and the circuit structure must preserve the query guarantees. Conditional SPNs combine conditional density estimation with circuit structure.

    A neural feature extractor before the circuit changes the modeled variable if it is non-invertible. Exact likelihood in feature space is not exact likelihood of raw observations. If the encoder is invertible, its Jacobian must be included; if it is stochastic, the result becomes a latent-variable model with an inference problem.

    Peer-reviewed research: Autoencoding Probabilistic Circuits, accepted by TMLR in 2025, jointly model data and probabilistic embeddings, infer embeddings with circuit queries, and attach a neural decoder using differentiable sampling. The paper reports reconstruction and missing-data robustness for its protocols. The tractable PC encoder does not make every neural-decoder likelihood or raw-space posterior query exact; the complete hybrid must be decomposed into its circuit and neural claims.

    Flow and Circuit Hybrids¤

    Probabilistic Flow Circuits augment circuit components with normalizing flows. Decomposability remains necessary to keep the combined model tractable. A flow can make leaf or component densities flexible, while the circuit supplies mixtures, factorizations, and exact high-level queries.

    This is hybrid composition, not an algebraic equivalence between PCs and flows. The flow handles a change of variables; the circuit handles reuse of sum/product structure.

    If a flow leaf covers scope \(S\), its change-of-variables density must be evaluated and marginalized only for queries the leaf supports. A full-scope flow leaf can give a tractable complete density yet destroy cheap arbitrary-subset marginals. "Every leaf has a likelihood" is weaker than "every leaf answers the marginal interface required by the parent circuit."

    Distillation from Deep Generative Models¤

    A teacher VAE, flow, or other deep model can suggest latent assignments or synthetic data for circuit learning. Liu et al. use Transformer-derived latent assignments and report substantial likelihood improvements over PCs trained without that supervision. Distillation does not transfer every teacher behavior or imply teacher dominance/reversal: conditioning, calibration, rare modes, and queries absent from the distillation loss require independent validation.

    Structured and Relational Domains¤

    Circuits extend beyond fixed image/tabular tensors. Sum-Product-Set Networks combine set semantics with circuits for tree-structured graphs. Logical circuits represent constraints exactly; tensor-network views expose connections to low-rank decompositions. Each extension must define variable scope and normalization for variable-size objects.

    The Sum-Product-Set work and Probabilistic Graph Circuits are preprint/workshop research as verified on 2026-07-12. Permutation invariance, graph validity, variable size, and tractable queries are separate requirements. Padding a graph into a fixed tensor can recover a conventional PC only if masks, node identity, and normalization semantics are explicit.

    The Random Graphs and Relational Generators volume places probabilistic graph circuits beside classical random-graph laws and other neural compositions, and separates exact circuit queries from graph-isomorphism and variable-cardinality requirements.

    Connections to Other Families¤

    • Mixtures/VAEs: sum nodes are discrete latent choices; exact circuit inference replaces a learned approximate encoder when structure permits.
    • Autoregressive models: both can have exact likelihood, but AR models normalize local conditionals while PCs compile reusable mixture/factorization structure.
    • Flows: both can evaluate exact densities under constraints; flows use invertible geometry, PCs use algebraic structure.
    • EBMs: a normalized PC can be written as energy \(E=-\log p\), but generic EBM composition does not preserve circuit tractability.
    • GFlowNets: a circuit sampling trace is a constructive process, but ordinary circuit MLE and GFlowNet reward-proportional training are different objectives.
    • Tensor-network and Born models: fixing a contraction order produces a sum-product circuit, while squared amplitudes produce squared circuits with different marginalization costs. The complete tensor/Born volume distinguishes circuit scopes and structural properties from MPS bond rank, gauges, and variable order.
    • Probabilistic grammars: an inside chart for one observation is an equivalent finite sum-product circuit under the chosen semiring; the recursive grammar is the reusable family-level specification, and cycles or unbounded length can prevent a finite tractable compilation. See Probabilistic Grammars and Program Derivations.

    Logical, tensor-network, and causal views¤

    Indicator leaves and deterministic sums make a PC resemble a weighted logical case decomposition. Compiling a Boolean theory into a deterministic structured circuit can make weighted model counting and constrained probabilistic queries efficient in the compiled size. The compilation step may be expensive, and an inconsistent theory gives zero mass rather than a usable conditional.

    Products and sums also resemble tensor contractions and low-rank factorizations. Einsum Networks exploit this computational view, but tensor rank, circuit width, vtree, and probabilistic normalization are different notions. A fast contraction order does not by itself supply normalized leaves or query-compatible scopes.

    Tensor-Network and Born Probability Models develops the other side of this relationship: nonnegative and amplitude-squared probability semantics, MPS canonical forms, exact conditional contractions, and the structural assumptions under which tensor factorizations and circuits support the same queries.

    Causal use requires another semantics layer. A joint observational PC can answer \(p(y\mid x)\); it does not identify \(p(y\mid\operatorname{do}(x))\). Causal circuits must encode or compose mechanisms and retain the structural properties required by the target interventional/counterfactual query. The relationship is hybrid system composition, not “tractable conditional equals causal effect.”

    Worked example: a flow leaf that preserves likelihood but loses a query¤

    Suppose a root mixture has two children, each a two-dimensional normalizing flow over \((X,Y)\). Complete density evaluation is tractable:

    \[ p(x,y)=\sum_{h=1}^2 w_h p_h(x,y). \]

    But the circuit query \(p(Y=y)=\sum_h w_h\int p_h(x,y)dx\) is tractable only if each flow component supplies that marginal. A generic coupled two-dimensional flow does not. Replacing independent univariate leaves with a full-scope flow increased local expressivity while breaking the leaf marginal interface on which Chapter 3 relied.

    One repair is to use decomposable flow leaves over separate scopes; another is a special flow family with tractable required marginals; a third is approximate integration, which must be labeled approximate. The parent sum/product graph alone cannot restore a query the leaf cannot answer.

    Counterexample: exact embeddings are not exact reconstructions

    A circuit may compute \(p(z\mid x_{\mathrm{obs}})\) exactly while a neural decoder maps \(z\) to a reconstruction. That output can be useful, but unless the decoder defines and supports the required normalized likelihood/inference operations, reconstruction is not an exact sample from a tractable raw-data conditional.

    Independent practice and assessment map: deep and hybrid circuits¤

    Attach every exactness claim to a variable, measure, component interface, and query. Treat a neural, flow, logical, tensor, or causal component as an added contract rather than as a name that inherits all PC guarantees.

    P1 — Preserve or reject a hybrid's query guarantees¤

    1. P1.1 — Predict before computing. Classify each claim as valid, assumption-bounded, or false: universal representability implies polynomial-size representation; a non-invertible encoder preserves raw-space exact likelihood; an invertible encoder needs no Jacobian when followed by a PC; a full-scope flow leaf with exact density automatically supports arbitrary marginals; distillation transfers every teacher conditional; a normalized PC written as \(-\log p\) makes generic EBM composition tractable; and an observational conditional identifies an intervention. Name the missing size, measure, interface, evidence, or causal assumption.
    2. P1.2 — Expressivity versus query cost. Construct a small overlapping mixture and a deterministic case decomposition for the same finite support. Count edges or cases, list the marginals and maximization queries each representation supports, and explain why one finite example does not prove or refute a worst-case size separation. State what an approximation theorem must specify about divergence, error, target class, and circuit restrictions.
    3. P1.3 — Flow-leaf interface test. Replace the univariate leaves of a decomposable circuit first with independent one-dimensional flows and then with a coupled full-scope flow. For complete likelihood, one-coordinate marginal, conditional sampling, and circuit product, write the exact leaf method required. Implement or specify a Gaussian/affine control whose subset marginal is known and reject any hybrid configuration whose required method is absent.
    4. P1.4 — Encoder/decoder density ledger. Compare four front ends: a fixed non-invertible encoder, an invertible flow encoder, a stochastic latent encoder, and a jointly modeled circuit embedding. For each, declare the variable with a normalized density, the Jacobian or inference term, reconstruction semantics, and supported raw-space conditionals. Design one test that catches the false promotion of exact feature-space inference to exact raw-space generation.
    5. P1.5 — Relationship and evidence audit. Choose one circuit–flow result, one representation-learning result, and one expressivity result cited in the chapter. Record publication maturity, modeled variable, circuit properties, teacher/encoder/decoder, query set, size/compute budget, dataset/split, metric, uncertainty, and reproduction status. Classify each cross-family statement as algebraic identity, hybrid composition, representational comparison, empirical result, or unsupported equivalence.
    6. P1.6 — Transfer. Transfer a tabular PC/flow hybrid to either permutation-invariant molecular sets or variable-size relational graphs. Redefine object support, cardinality law, symmetries, masks/identities, normalization, leaf interfaces, and validity queries. State which fixed-tensor circuit guarantees survive and which require set, graph, logical, or approximate machinery.

    Chapter 5 Synthesis¤

    Deep and hybrid PCs increase local expressivity while circuit structure protects only the queries implemented by every component. Worst-case approximation lower bounds explain why extra tractability can require extra size. Every hybrid must say which variable has exact density, which constraints survive, and which costs move into leaves, encoders, decoders, or compilation.

    Retrieval practice: expressivity and hybrids
    1. What is expressive efficiency, and why is it different from universal approximation?
    2. Does exact likelihood after an arbitrary neural encoder apply to raw data?
    3. What interface must a leaf expose for arbitrary missing-data marginalization?
    4. What does the 2025 approximation-hardness result establish?
    5. How should the 2026 PC–LLM expressivity paper be labeled?

    Solutions. (1) It asks how circuit size grows to represent or approximate a distribution under structural constraints; a class may be universal yet require exponentially large members. (2) No. A non-invertible encoder discards information/volume; an invertible encoder needs its Jacobian; a stochastic encoder creates latent inference. (3) Exact integrals/conditionals for the subsets requested by parent operations, not only complete density. (4) Worst-case hardness and size separations persist under bounded approximation error for the studied classes/divergences. (5) Preprint research, with assumptions and finite experiments separated from established results.
