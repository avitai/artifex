    3. Exponential Families, Mirror Geometry, and Variational Inference¤

    Chapter 3 prerequisites and learning objectives¤

    Prerequisites. Exponential families, conjugacy, the ELBO, and stochastic variational inference.

    By the end, you should be able to:

    1. O1 — Derive dual coordinates: compute mean parameters and Fisher information from the log-partition function.
    2. O2 — Translate natural motion: convert gradients between natural and mean coordinates without identifying the coordinate arrays.
    3. O3 — Interpret moment matching: derive maximum-likelihood natural flow as movement of sufficient-statistic means.
    4. O4 — Execute conjugate SVI: form an unbiased minibatch target and a natural-coordinate averaging update.
    5. O5 — Audit amortized geometry: distinguish variational-conditional, generative-joint, and observed-model Fishers in a VAE.
    6. O6 — Bound structural transfer: state which conjugacy, minimality, mixture, quotient, or shared-parameter assumptions a claimed natural update requires.

    Dual coordinates¤

    For a minimal regular exponential family

    \[ q_\eta(z)=h(z)\exp\{\eta^\top T(z)-A(\eta)\}, \]

    the mean parameter and Fisher information are

    \[ \mu=\mathbb E_{q_\eta}[T(Z)]=\nabla_\eta A(\eta), \qquad F_\eta=\nabla_\eta^2A(\eta) =\operatorname{Cov}_{q_\eta}[T(Z)]. \]

    If a differentiable objective is written as \(L(\eta)=\widetilde L(\mu(\eta))\), then the chain rule gives

    \[ \nabla_\eta L=F_\eta\nabla_\mu\widetilde L, \qquad \widetilde\nabla_\eta L =F_\eta^{-1}\nabla_\eta L =\nabla_\mu\widetilde L. \]

    Natural and mean coordinates are dual; they are not numerically identical arrays with interchangeable semantics. This duality connects natural-gradient steps, Bregman proximal updates generated by \(A\), and mirror descent. The equivalence must name its coordinate, objective, and finite-step convention.

    Maximum likelihood as moment motion¤

    For fully observed data with empirical statistic \(\bar T\), the average negative log likelihood is

    \[ J(\eta)=A(\eta)-\eta^\top\bar T+\text{constant}, \]

    so \(\nabla_\eta J=\mu-\bar T\). Infinitesimally, a natural-gradient flow produces

    \[ \dot\mu=-F_\eta F_\eta^{-1}(\mu-\bar T) =\bar T-\mu. \]

    The mean parameter moves directly toward the empirical moment. A finite Euler step in \(\eta\) is not necessarily the same as an exact convex interpolation in \(\mu\), because the coordinate map is nonlinear.

    Conjugate SVI: when the update becomes simple¤

    In the conjugate models treated by Hoffman et al., let \(\lambda\) be a global variational natural parameter and let \(\widehat\lambda\) be the complete-data conjugate update formed from prior hyperparameters plus expected sufficient statistics. Under the paper's factorization and unbiased minibatch construction, the natural gradient of the ELBO with respect to \(\lambda\) has the form

    \[ \widetilde\nabla_\lambda\mathcal L =\widehat\lambda-\lambda. \]

    The stochastic update is therefore

    \[ \lambda_{t+1} =(1-\rho_t)\lambda_t+\rho_t\widehat\lambda_t. \]

    This is not a universal identity for arbitrary amortized neural posteriors. It depends on conjugate exponential-family structure and the chosen global variational coordinate.

    Worked Beta--Bernoulli minibatch update¤

    Suppose a Bernoulli probability has prior \(\operatorname{Beta}(2,2)\). There are \(N=100\) observations. A uniformly sampled minibatch of size \(10\) contains seven successes and three failures. Scaling the minibatch counts by \(N/10\) gives the stochastic full-data target. Here we use the concentration pair \(\lambda=(\alpha,\beta)\), which is an affine shift of the canonical natural coordinate \((\alpha-1,\beta-1)\) and therefore obeys the same convex update:

    \[ \widehat\lambda=(2+70,\ 2+30)=(72,32). \]

    Starting from \(\lambda_t=(12,8)\) and using \(\rho_t=0.1\) gives

    \[ \lambda_{t+1} =0.9(12,8)+0.1(72,32) =(18,10.4). \]

    The minibatch target is noisy but unbiased under uniform sampling. Reusing a nonuniform data stream without importance correction changes that claim.

    VAEs and amortization change the geometric object¤

    A VAE contains at least two parameterized distribution families:

    1. the generative joint \(p_\theta(x,z)\); and
    2. the variational conditional \(q_\phi(z\mid x)\).

    A natural gradient for \(q_\phi\) can use the conditional Fisher

    \[ F_\phi^{(q)} =\mathbb E_{X\sim p_D} \mathbb E_{Z\sim q_\phi(\cdot\mid X)} \left[s_\phi(Z\mid X)s_\phi(Z\mid X)^\top\right]. \]

    That is not the Fisher of the decoder distribution. Moreover, shared amortized parameters couple the posterior updates for every record, so one neural step cannot generally reproduce independent coordinate-optimal updates for all \(q(z\mid x)\).

    Ay, van Oostrum, and Datar analyze the Fisher--Rao gradient of the ELBO and identify conditions under which ELBO and target-KL geometry align on a constrained model. The result is a geometric characterization, not permission to treat every finite VAE optimizer as exact posterior natural-gradient descent. Natural-gradient VI also extends beyond minimal single-family approximations, but mixture and structured cases need their own representations and algorithms, as shown by Lin, Khan, and Schmidt.

    Independent practice and assessment map: dual coordinates and variational geometry¤

    Use exact exponential families as the calculation laboratory, then state which identities survive when amortization, mixtures, or nonuniform sampling are introduced.

    1. P1.1 — Derive Bernoulli dual coordinates. Type: derive · Difficulty: core · Typical time: 10 min. Write Bernoulli probability \(q\) as a minimal exponential family with natural coordinate \(\eta\). Derive \(A(\eta)\), mean coordinate \(\mu\), and Fisher \(F_\eta\).
    2. P1.2 — Predict before computing: moment motion. Type: predict · Difficulty: foundation · Typical time: 8 min. For target frequency \(r\), predict whether an infinitesimal natural-gradient flow in \(\eta\) makes \(\mu\) approach \(r\) linearly. Derive the result and contrast it with an ordinary gradient step in \(\eta\).
    3. P1.3 — Execute a conjugate SVI update. Type: derive · Difficulty: core · Typical time: 10 min. A \(\operatorname{Beta}(1,1)\) prior observes \(N=50\) Bernoulli records. A uniform minibatch of five contains four successes. From current concentration pair \((6,4)\), compute the full-data stochastic target and the update with \(\rho=0.2\).
    4. P1.4 — Repair nonuniform minibatch bias. Type: diagnose · Difficulty: stretch · Typical time: 12 min. Four records have success statistics \((1,0,1,1)\) and are sampled one at a time with probabilities \((0.4,0.1,0.2,0.3)\). Compare the expectation of naive estimator \(4T_I\) with importance-corrected estimator \(T_I/\pi_I\) for the full success count.
    5. P1.5 — Select a VAE Fisher. Type: compare · Difficulty: core · Typical time: 12 min. A proposal says “use the VAE Fisher” to update only encoder parameters. Give three candidate Fishers, choose the one that directly measures local change of the amortized posterior, and state the averaging law over observations.
    6. P1.6 — Transfer beyond a minimal family. Type: design · Difficulty: stretch · Typical time: 17 min. Consider a two-component Gaussian mixture variational family with exchangeable component labels. Explain why blindly inverting a minimal-family Fisher is invalid. Specify the quotient or identifiability issue, an algorithmic representation, and diagnostics for label switching and singular components.

    Complete solutions to P1
    1. P1.1. \(p_\eta(x)=\exp\{\eta x-A(\eta)\}\) on \(x\in\{0,1\}\) with \(A(\eta)=\log(1+e^\eta)\). Therefore \(\mu=\nabla A=\sigma(\eta)=q\) and \(F_\eta=\nabla^2A=q(1-q)=\operatorname{Var}(X)\). Check. Minimality holds on the interior because the sufficient statistic is not affinely redundant.
    2. P1.2. Cross-entropy has ordinary logit gradient \(g_\eta=\mu-r\). The natural direction is \((\mu-r)/[\mu(1-\mu)]\), so descent induces \(\dot\mu=(d\mu/d\eta)\dot\eta=r-\mu\). Ordinary logit descent instead induces \(\dot\mu=-\mu(1-\mu)(\mu-r)\) and slows near the boundary. Check. The linear moment flow is infinitesimal; a finite Euler step in \(\eta\) is not exact linear interpolation in \(\mu\).
    3. P1.3. Scaling by \(50/5=10\) gives estimated counts \((40,10)\), so the posterior target is \((1+40,1+10)=(41,11)\). The natural-coordinate averaging update is \(0.8(6,4)+0.2(41,11)=(13,5.4)\). Check. Concentration and canonical coordinates differ only by the fixed affine shift used consistently here.
    4. P1.4. The true full success count is three. The naive expectation is \(\sum_i\pi_i4T_i=4(0.4+0.2+0.3)=3.6\), so uniform scaling is biased. The corrected expectation is \(\sum_i\pi_iT_i/\pi_i=\sum_iT_i=3\). Check. Zero-statistic records contribute zero but still belong to the sampling law.
    5. P1.5. Candidates include the generative-joint Fisher of \(p_\theta(x,z)\), the observed-marginal Fisher of \(p_\theta(x)\), and the variational-conditional Fisher of \(q_\phi(z\mid x)\). For an encoder-only local posterior change, use \(F_\phi^{(q)}=\mathbb E_{X\sim r_X}\mathbb E_{Z\sim q_\phi(\cdot\mid X)}[s_\phi s_\phi^\top]\), with \(r_X\) declared as training data, reweighted data, or deployment law. Check. A GGN may be useful but is not renamed as this conditional Fisher.
    6. P1.6. Permuting two component labels leaves the mixture distribution unchanged, and coincident or vanishing components create additional singular directions. A defensible method uses an ordered identifiable chart, a quotient-aware construction, or a published mixture-specific natural-gradient representation with component responsibilities. Report effective component mass, Fisher spectrum on identifiable directions, label-aligned law movement, and behavior near merging components. Rubric. Adding \(\lambda I\) may stabilize a solve but does not remove the quotient ambiguity.

    Chapter 3 synthesis and retrieval practice¤

    Exponential-family duality can make a natural gradient look like a first-order moment update. Conjugate SVI exploits that fact exactly within its model class; amortized neural inference introduces a shared parameterization and a different finite problem.

    Retrieval questions
    1. Why is \(F_\eta=\nabla^2A(\eta)\)?
    2. What makes \(\widehat\lambda-\lambda\) a natural rather than ordinary gradient in conjugate SVI?
    3. Which Fisher would an encoder natural gradient use in a VAE?

    Solutions. (1) The first derivative of \(A\) is the sufficient-statistic mean and the second is its covariance. (2) The variational parameter is a conjugate exponential-family natural coordinate whose Fisher converts the ordinary derivative to that difference. (3) The Fisher of \(q_\phi(z\mid x)\) under a declared distribution over \(x\), not automatically the Fisher of \(p_\theta(x,z)\).

    Bridge. Natural geometry does not decide how to differentiate through a random latent variable. Chapter 4 studies the estimator layer before returning to model-scale metric computation.
