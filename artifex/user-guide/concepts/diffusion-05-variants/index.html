    Diffusion Model Variants¤

    Diffusion Model Variants: Prerequisites and learning objectives¤

    By the end of this chapter, you should be able to:

    1. specify diffusion on observed pixels, learned continuous latents, discrete tokens, graphs, manifolds, functions, video, and 3D representations;
    2. identify the base measure and corruption kernel for each;
    3. distinguish conditional generation from inverse-problem posterior sampling; and
    4. account for every codec, cascade, renderer, teacher, and guidance component in a hybrid system.

    The geometric and equivariant generation guide explains when centered, tangent, categorical, or manifold noise is required and why an equivariant network alone does not make the full diffusion process equivariant.

    For discrete graph diffusion, Random Graphs and Relational Generators makes the endpoint graph law, relabeling convention, sparsity regime, node-count process, constraints, and graph-specific evaluation explicit.

    Copula Models explains why ordinary Gaussian noising is not a copula path and how marginal-preserving processes can instead remove and restore dependence while retaining uniform coordinates.

    Latent Diffusion Models (LDM / Stable Diffusion)¤

    Latent Diffusion (Rombach et al., 2022 — CVPR) makes the learned representation part of the generative system: a codec maps observations to a lower-dimensional latent space, diffusion models the latent distribution, and a decoder returns to observation space.

    This speed–fidelity result depends on the codec's rate, distortion, support, scaling, and retained semantics. The cross-family Representations and Multimodal Generative Systems volume makes those dependencies explicit and connects them to discrete tokenizers, temporal codecs, fusion, and AR–diffusion hybrids.

    Two-Stage Approach:

    1. Train autoencoder compressing images 8× (512×512×3 → 64×64×4)
    2. Run diffusion in compressed latent space

    Architecture:

    Key Benefits:

    • The LDM paper reports favorable compute–quality trade-offs for selected compression settings; no universal speedup transfers across codecs or hardware
    • Smaller latent grids can reduce denoiser activation and attention cost, while the codec adds reconstruction error and encode/decode cost
    • Cross-attention is one conditioning mechanism used by the text-conditional LDM system, not a consequence of latent modeling

    Stable Diffusion Implementation:

    • 860M-parameter U-Net in latent space
    • Trained on LAION-5B (5 billion text-image pairs)
    • Public checkpoints and code enabled broad study, adaptation, and deployment; each release retains its own license, data, and safety constraints
    • SD 1.x, SD 2.x, SDXL, SD3, and distilled systems share parts of a latent pipeline but differ in codec, target, backbone, conditioning, data, post-training, and sampler

    SDXL (Podell et al., 2023):

    • 2.6B-parameter UNet (≈ 3× SD 1.5)
    • Dual text encoders (OpenCLIP-G + CLIP-L) concatenated
    • Native 1024×1024 training and generation
    • Two-stage: base model + refiner

    Stable Diffusion 3 / SD3.5 (Esser et al., 2024 — MMDiT):

    • Rectified-flow Transformer (MMDiT) replacing U-Net
    • Multimodal Diffusion Transformer with separate streams for text and image tokens
    • Sizes: 800M, 2B, 8B parameters
    • The paper reports improved typography and prompt-following comparisons for the complete SD3 system; those gains cannot be assigned to MMDiT alone without a controlled ablation

    FLUX.1 (Black Forest Labs, 2024):

    • 12B-parameter rectified-flow transformer (the largest open-weights image model at release).
    • Hybrid multimodal + parallel-DiT blocks with rotary positional embeddings.
    • The disclosed FLUX.1 variants differ in model access and distillation. The schnell technical materials describe one- to four-step latent adversarial diffusion distillation; current availability and license terms belong in release documentation, not in the model-family definition. See Distillation, Flow Matching, and Discrete State Spaces.
    • Community leaderboard rankings were protocol- and snapshot-dependent; use archived dates, prompts, judge details, and matched sampling settings rather than a timeless rank.

    System boundary

    A latent-diffusion checkpoint, codec, text encoder, guidance rule, scheduler, safety layer, and fine-tune are separate components. Model-family claims should not be inferred from ecosystem size or product adoption.

    Conditional Diffusion Models¤

    Class-Conditional Generation:

    Add class label \(y\) as conditioning:

    \[ p_\theta(x_{t-1} | x_t, y) = \mathcal{N}(x_{t-1}; \mu_\theta(x_t, t, y), \Sigma_\theta(x_t, t, y)) \]

    Typically implemented via:

    • Class embeddings concatenated with time embeddings
    • Conditional batch normalization: Modulate batch norm with class info
    • Cross-attention: Attend to class token

    Text-to-Image Models:

    DALL·E 2 (Ramesh et al., 2022 — unCLIP):

    • Two-stage: CLIP prior diffusion + decoder.
    • CLIP prior maps text embeddings to image embeddings.
    • Decoder is a 64×64 diffusion model + two cascaded super-resolution diffusion models (→ 256² → 1024²).
    • Successors: DALL·E 3 (Betker et al., 2023) — improves prompt following via better caption supervision.

    Imagen (Saharia et al., 2022):

    • Frozen T5-XXL (4.6B parameters) as text encoder.
    • Cascaded diffusion: 64×64 → 256×256 → 1024×1024.
    • Key finding: scaling the text encoder improves quality more than scaling the U-Net.
    • The paper reports FID 7.27 on its COCO evaluation protocol; the number is meaningful only with its captioning, sampling, feature implementation, and comparison set.
    • Imagen 3 (Google, 2024) reports improved photorealism and text rendering under its evaluation protocol.

    Midjourney and other closed systems disclose insufficient architecture, data, and training detail for precise family classification across versions. Treat interface behavior and vendor claims separately from reproducible diffusion evidence.

    Cascade Diffusion Models¤

    Generate through multiple resolution stages, each a separate diffusion model:

    1. Base model: Generate at 64×64
    2. Super-resolution 1: Upscale to 256×256
    3. Super-resolution 2: Upscale to 1024×1024

    Advantages:

    • Each stage focuses on different detail scales
    • More efficient than single high-resolution model
    • Better quality through specialized models

    Disadvantages:

    • Complexity of training multiple models
    • Error accumulation across stages

    Used in DALL·E 2 and Imagen.

    Video Diffusion Models¤

    Video diffusion extends spatial generation to the temporal dimension. Two architectural lineages dominate:

    • 3-D U-Net (Imagen Video, AnimateDiff, Stable Video Diffusion): inflate a 2-D U-Net's spatial convolutions to spatio-temporal, with factorised space-time attention — see Spatial, Temporal, and 3-D Attention.
    • Spacetime DiT (Sora, HunyuanVideo, Wan, Mochi-1, CogVideoX, LTX-Video): patchify the video at \((t, h, w)\) resolution and run a transformer over the joint spatio-temporal token sequence.

    Transformer-based stacks are used by many large-scale video models, including Sora, while U-Net-style systems remain important where their inductive biases or deployment profile fit the workload.

    Sora (Brooks et al., OpenAI, 2024):

    • Diffusion Transformer on spacetime patches — videos compressed into a lower-dimensional latent, then patchified like a ViT.
    • Up to 1 minute of 1080p video, variable aspect ratios.
    • The report presents qualitative examples of learned scene regularities alongside failures; these are predictive visual behaviors, not evidence of a causal or physically correct world model.

    Sora 2 (OpenAI, 2025):

    • Synchronized audio generation.
    • Improved physics simulation, multi-shot continuity.
    • Instruction-following for long, complex scenes.

    Veo and Movie Gen (Polyak et al., Meta, 2024) illustrate closed- or restricted-weight video systems. Their reports do not expose enough matched training, data, and inference detail for timeless cross-system rankings.

    Open-source video model examples:

    • HunyuanVideo (Tencent, 2024) — 13B-parameter open foundation video model; outperformed Luma 1.6 and several closed Chinese models on human-eval text alignment, motion quality, and visual quality.
    • CogVideoX (Yang et al., 2024) — 2B and 5B parameter expert-MoE diffusion models with 3-D causal-conv VAE.
    • Mochi-1 (Genmo, 2024) — 10B asymmetric DiT (AsymmDiT) with an 8×8 spatial / 6× temporal VAE; 480p, 5.4 s, Apache-2.0.
    • Wan 2.1 / 2.2 (Alibaba, 2025) — later versions use timestep-specialized experts. Quality rankings require a named benchmark version, prompt set, evaluator, resolution, duration, and verification date.
    • LTX-Video (Lightricks, 2025) — real-time video generation distilled to few steps.
    • Stable Video Diffusion (Blattmann et al., 2023) — image-to-video extension of SD.

    3D Diffusion Models¤

    DreamFusion (Poole et al., 2022):

    Uses 2D text-to-image models as priors for 3D generation via Score Distillation Sampling (SDS):

    1. Initialize random 3D NeRF
    2. Render 2D views from random camera angles
    3. Apply noise and use Imagen to denoise
    4. Backpropagate through rendering to update NeRF
    5. Repeat

    Key innovation: the optimization uses a pretrained 2D diffusion prior without a task-specific 3D training set, but it inherits that prior's data and biases and does not identify hidden geometry uniquely.

    Capabilities:

    • Text-to-3D generation
    • Viewable from any angle
    • Relightable
    • Exportable as meshes

    Stable-DreamFusion:

    • Uses Stable Diffusion instead of Imagen
    • Open-source implementation
    • Enables text-to-3D and image-to-3D

    Applications: Game assets, VR/AR content, product design, 3D scene reconstruction

    Worked example: the codec changes the modeled distribution¤

    Suppose an encoder maps two distinct images \(x^{(1)}\) and \(x^{(2)}\) to the same latent \(z\). A perfect latent diffusion model can reproduce the latent distribution exactly, yet its decoder cannot recover both originals deterministically from \(z\). Pixel-space fidelity is therefore bounded by codec information loss before denoising error is considered. An honest system evaluation reports codec reconstruction distortion, prior sample quality, decoder cost, latent scaling, and end-to-end metrics separately.

    Independent practice and assessment map: state spaces and variants¤

    Define the state, measure, corruption, endpoint, reverse object, and renderer before reusing diffusion vocabulary across modalities. Evaluate every learned representation and auxiliary component as part of the generative system.

    P1 — Rebuild diffusion after changing its state space¤

    1. P1.1 — Predict before computing. Predict whether each statement is necessarily true: a perfect latent prior yields perfect observations; an equivariant denoiser makes an arbitrary Euclidean noising process valid on a manifold; Gaussian noise is a valid categorical kernel; a cascade is one normalized model without further specification; SDS is exact posterior sampling from a normalized 3D model; and conditioning on a measurement is equivalent to unconditional generation followed by visual selection. Name the missing system component or probabilistic construction.
    2. P1.2 — State-space contract matrix. For observed pixels, continuous codec latents, categorical tokens, variable-node graphs, a Riemannian manifold, functions on irregular coordinates, video, and molecular 3D structures, specify support, base measure, forward kernel or path, reference law, reverse target, symmetry or constraint, and numerical sampler. Mark which Gaussian identities survive, require tangent or equivariant reformulation, or fail entirely.
    3. P1.3 — Codec and component accounting. Construct a finite encoder that maps four observations to two latent codes and a stochastic or deterministic decoder. Compute the best attainable reconstruction behavior and show how two different observation laws can induce the same latent law. Then produce an end-to-end call, memory, latency, and error ledger for encoder, latent generator, guidance branches, decoder, cascade or renderer, and safety/reranking stages.
    4. P1.4 — Conditional versus posterior generation. For a linear observation model with known noise, distinguish training a conditional diffusion model \(p_\theta(x\mid y)\) from combining an unconditional prior with a likelihood at inference. State the target distribution, required paired data, approximation points, calibration checks, and failure modes of each. Explain why visually plausible measurement-inconsistent samples fail the inverse-problem contract.
    5. P1.5 — Transfer. Design a diffusion system for protein backbones, molecular docking poses, or irregular climate fields. Declare chirality or coordinate symmetries, masks and variable size, base measure, corruption kernel, conditional information, codec or representation, reverse target, solver, physical constraints, and uncertainty evaluation. Include a tractable small system and matched autoregressive or flow baseline that could falsify the claimed advantage.

    Diffusion Model Variants: Chapter synthesis and retrieval practice¤

    “Diffusion” specifies neither the state space nor a complete product. Learned representations, discrete kernels, geometry, cascades, renderers, conditioning, and post-training determine what distribution is actually approximated.

    Retrieval practice: diffusion variants
    1. Why can perfect latent-prior learning still yield imperfect images?
    2. What must replace Gaussian noise for categorical states?
    3. Why does an equivariant denoiser not by itself make a manifold diffusion valid?
    4. Is SDS posterior sampling from a normalized 3D model by default?

    Solutions. (1) The codec may be lossy or map distinct observations to the same latent. (2) A declared categorical transition, masking/absorbing process, or CTMC with the correct support. (3) The forward kernel, measure, tangent projection, reverse dynamics, and solver must also respect the geometry. (4) No; it is an optimization objective using a 2D prior unless an explicit normalized 3D probabilistic construction and sampler are supplied.

    Bridge to Chapter 6. The final technical chapter relates diffusion to probability paths, flow matching, stochastic interpolants, Schrödinger bridges, EDM, and few-step transitions while preserving the assumptions that keep those correspondences honest.
