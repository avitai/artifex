    Summary and Key Takeaways¤

    VAEs combine neural latent-variable models with variational inference. They can learn useful representations, but structure, interpretability, identifiability, and disentanglement require additional assumptions and empirical validation. Understanding VAEs provides essential foundations for modern generative modeling, from continuous latent codecs used by latent diffusion to discrete VQ-style codes used by image and video tokenizers.

    Capstone objectives¤

    After completing this chapter, you should be able to:

    1. specify a latent generative story and observation likelihood;
    2. derive and implement its variational objective;
    3. choose diagonal, structured, or temporal posterior dependencies for a declared query;
    4. diagnose approximation, amortization, and model gaps;
    5. decide whether a proposed “VAE” is a probabilistic generator or codec component; and
    6. design an evidence-bounded experiment through deployment accounting.

    Core Principles:

    • ELBO objective trades expected observation log likelihood against posterior-to-prior rate; “latent structure” is not a guaranteed term
    • Reparameterization trick supplies a pathwise gradient estimator for compatible distributions; other estimators remain possible
    • Probabilistic framework provides an explicit latent-variable model and posterior approximation; whether its latent geometry is smooth, covered, or semantically useful must be measured
    • Variational inference provides principled approximations to intractable posteriors

    Key Variants:

    • β-VAE / β-TCVAE / FactorVAE alter rate and dependence penalties to encourage particular factorisations; disentanglement and interpretability remain assumption- and metric-dependent
    • InfoVAE / MMD-VAE / AAE separate or estimate aggregate-prior matching through different objectives; matching one marginal does not determine per-record information
    • VQ-VAE / VQGAN use discrete latents and expose a code sequence to a separate prior; quality and utilization remain empirical
    • Conditional VAE enables controlled generation with auxiliary information
    • Hierarchical VAEs allocate stochastic information across groups; scale semantics and improvements must be measured
    • Sequence VAEs may use one persistent code, per-time stochastic states, or both; filtering, smoothing, and autoregressive generation remain distinct computations
    • Latent-generator codecs may be KL-regularized autoencoders, VQ systems, or foundation-aligned tokenizers; the codec and downstream prior are distinct components

    Best Practices:

    • Begin with the intended objective and no-annealing baseline; ablate schedules rather than prescribing them by modality
    • Monitor likelihood/reconstruction, total and group KL, active units, prior samples, and downstream utility
    • Match observation distribution, support, scale, and reduction to the data
    • Treat perceptual/adversarial losses as changed objectives with their own fidelity, coverage, and calibration consequences
    • Separate codec, posterior, prior, and full-system evaluations

    Cumulative research exercise¤

    Design a latent model for probabilistic weather-field forecasting with calibrated uncertainty, irregular missing observations, physical conservation diagnostics, and fast ensemble sampling.

    Worked solution: one defensible design

    Define a conditional latent state-space model rather than a plain image VAE: \(p(z_{1:T}\mid c)\) for dynamics and \(p(x_t\mid z_t,c)\) for observation sites, with masks included in the likelihood rather than imputed as truth. Use an amortized smoother \(q(z_{1:T}\mid x_{\mathrm{obs}},c)\) and report its ELBO, importance estimates, posterior predictive calibration, and amortization gap on small cases where stronger inference is available. Encode physical constraints either through state coordinates, equivariant/conservative architecture, or explicit penalties, then report violations rather than claiming they are guaranteed. Compare direct decoder ensembles with an iterative latent prior under matched wall-clock and sample count. This is a stochastic-process/latent hybrid; a low reconstruction MSE alone would not validate forecast uncertainty.

    Independent practice and assessment map: synthesis¤

    Complete this capstone without reusing the weather-field solution. The answer must connect a probability law, inference query, executable validation plan, and evidence-qualified deployment decision.

    P1 — Design and challenge a multimodal intensive-care trajectory model¤

    Design a model for irregular vital signs, laboratory values, clinical events, and treatment context. It must support calibrated forecasts, missing modalities, fast scenario sampling, and a declared privacy and safety evaluation.

    1. P1.1 — Predict before computing. Predict whether calling the system a VAE guarantees calibrated uncertainty, useful patient-state coordinates, robustness to informative missingness, or valid treatment counterfactuals. For every prediction, name the absent assumption, query, or experiment.
    2. P1.2 — End-to-end derivation. Specify the conditional generative story, observation likelihoods and masks, global and/or temporal latents, prior, posterior factorization, and ELBO or alternative objective. Derive its per-record loss and identify every reduction, estimator, and sequential sampling dependency.
    3. P1.3 — Gap and evidence study. Design controls for model misspecification, posterior approximation, amortization, optimization, missingness shift, and decoder/prior composition. Report calibration, held-out bounds, posterior predictive checks, subgroup uncertainty, utility, privacy attacks, latency, memory, and uncertainty over seeds.
    4. P1.4 — Transfer and routing. Recast the system first as a deterministic codec plus an independently trained latent prior and then as an unordered set generator. State which probability, inference, symmetry, evaluation, and deployment claims change; route each implementation question to documentation, a local executable test, or a new scientific experiment.

    Final misconception check¤

    • A Gaussian prior does not guarantee a smooth, semantic, or fully covered latent space.
    • The ELBO is not exact likelihood unless the variational posterior gap vanishes.
    • Optimizing a restricted stochastic ELBO does not guarantee that training reached a local maximum-likelihood solution.
    • Posterior collapse is latent non-use; whether it is a failure depends on the modeling goal.
    • Aggregate-prior matching alone does not establish mutual information, semantic factors, or accurate per-example inference.
    • A discrete codebook avoids one Gaussian-collapse mechanism but can itself be underused.
    • Lower reconstruction error does not guarantee a better downstream generative tokenizer.
    • “VAE” in a product stack may denote a lightly regularized codec rather than an end-to-end variational generator.
    Retrieval practice: cumulative VAE synthesis
    1. Name the model, inference, approximation, and amortization gaps that can affect a VAE result.
    2. Why does optimizing \(\beta<1\) cease to be the ordinary ELBO?
    3. What evidence distinguishes a useful inferred latent from a merely reconstructive one?
    4. Which relationship label describes a codec followed by an independently trained latent diffusion prior?

    Solutions. (1) Model misspecification/data gap, variational-family approximation gap, amortization gap from shared inference, and finite optimization/estimation error. (2) It reweights the posterior-to-prior KL away from the variational identity for \(\log p_\theta(x)\). (3) Downstream probes/interventions, calibration or conditional utility, robustness, and comparison to baselines under held-out data—not reconstruction alone. (4) Hybrid composition.

Use the practical guide for live Artifex behavior, then study density/latent/energy relationships, representations and multimodal systems, diffusion priors, and research evaluation. Return to the ELBO derivation whenever a modern tokenizer is called a VAE: the name is justified only by the actual probabilistic objective.

    Seminal Papers (Must Read)¤

    Kingma, D. P., & Welling, M. (2013). "Auto-Encoding Variational Bayes"
         arXiv:1312.6114
         The original VAE paper introducing the framework and reparameterization trick

    Rezende, D. J., Mohamed, S., & Wierstra, D. (2014). "Stochastic Backpropagation and Approximate Inference in Deep Generative Models"
         arXiv:1401.4082
         Independent development of similar ideas with deep latent Gaussian models

    Higgins, I., et al. (2017). "β-VAE: Learning Basic Visual Concepts with a Constrained Variational Framework"
         ICLR 2017
         Introduces β-VAE for disentangled representations

    Van den Oord, A., Vinyals, O., & Kavukcuoglu, K. (2017). "Neural Discrete Representation Learning"
         arXiv:1711.00937
         VQ-VAE for discrete latent representations

    Tutorial Papers and Books¤

    Kingma, D. P., & Welling, M. (2019). "An Introduction to Variational Autoencoders"
         arXiv:1906.02691
         Detailed tutorial by the original authors

    Doersch, C. (2016). "Tutorial on Variational Autoencoders"
         arXiv:1606.05908
         Excellent intuitive introduction with minimal prerequisites

    Ghojogh, B., et al. (2021). "Factor Analysis, Probabilistic PCA, Variational Inference, and VAE: Tutorial and Survey"

    ELBO, Joint-Distribution, and Inference Analysis¤

    Hoffman, M. D., & Johnson, M. J. (2016). "ELBO Surgery: Yet Another Way to Carve Up the Variational Evidence Lower Bound" (workshop paper)
         AABI 2016 paper
         Decomposes the average per-example prior KL into mutual information and aggregated-posterior mismatch

    Cremer, C., Li, X., & Duvenaud, D. (2018). "Inference Suboptimality in Variational Autoencoders" (ICML)
         PMLR paper
         Separates approximation and amortization contributions to the inference gap

    Zhao, S., Song, J., & Ermon, S. (2019). "InfoVAE: Balancing Learning and Inference in Variational Autoencoders" (AAAI)
         AAAI paper
         Studies VAE learning through model and inference joints and alternative latent-marginal objectives

    Margossian, C. C., & Blei, D. M. (2024). "Amortized Variational Inference: When and Why?" (UAI)
         PMLR paper
         Gives model-dependent conditions for amortized inference to attain per-instance variational optima

    Conditional, Multimodal, and Semi-Supervised VAEs¤

    Sutter, T. M., Daunhawer, I., & Vogt, J. E. (2021). "Generalized Multimodal ELBO" (ICLR)

    Mancisidor, R. A., Jenssen, R., Yu, S., & Kampffmeyer, M. (2025). "Aggregation of Dependent Expert Distributions in Multimodal Variational Autoencoders" (ICML)

    Vo, H. K., & Valera, I. (2026). "Hellinger Multimodal Variational Autoencoders" (AISTATS)

    VAEs as Components in Generative Pipelines¤

    Rombach, R., et al. (2022). "High-Resolution Image Synthesis with Latent Diffusion Models" (Stable Diffusion)

    Esser, P., Rombach, R., & Ommer, B. (2021). "Taming Transformers for High-Resolution Image Synthesis" (VQGAN)

    Sadat, A., et al. (2024). "LiteVAE: Lightweight and Efficient Variational Autoencoders for Latent Diffusion Models"

    Zhao, S., et al. (2024). "CV-VAE: A Compatible Video VAE for Latent Generative Video Models"

    Li, Y., et al. (2024). "WF-VAE: Enhancing Video VAE by Wavelet-Driven Energy Flow"

    Kouzelis, T., et al. (2025). "EQ-VAE: Equivariance Regularized Latent Space for Improved Generative Image Modeling" (ICML 2025)
         arXiv:2502.09509

    Chen, H., et al. (2025). "Masked Autoencoders Are Effective Tokenizers for Diffusion Models" (MAETok)

    Yu, S., et al. (2025). "Reconstruction vs. Generation: Taming Optimization Dilemma in Latent Diffusion Models"
