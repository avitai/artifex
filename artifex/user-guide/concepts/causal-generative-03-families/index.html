    3. Deep Structural Generators: VAEs, Flows, GANs, and Diffusion¤

    Chapter 3 prerequisites and learning objectives¤

    Prerequisites. Chapters 1–2 and the VAE, flow, GAN, diffusion, autoregressive, and GFlowNet volumes.

    By the end, you should be able to:

    1. O1 — Separate semantics from parameterization: identify which claims come from the SCM and which come from a VAE, flow, GAN, diffusion, AR, EBM, or GFlowNet component.
    2. O2 — Match mechanisms to computational contracts: compare likelihood, sampling, posterior inference, invertibility, and abductive reuse.
    3. O3 — Execute modular interventions: replace one mechanism, regenerate descendants, and preserve the correct non-descendant and exogenous state.
    4. O4 — Distinguish population and unit generation: decide when to sample fresh noise and when a factual posterior must be retained.
    5. O5 — Diagnose neural pseudocausality: reject claims based only on prompting, latent editing, attention masks, realism, or observational fit.
    6. O6 — Design hybrid causal generators: combine families across mechanism boundaries while keeping identification and approximation errors explicit.

    Separate causal semantics from neural machinery¤

    A deep SCM replaces some structural assignments or noise models with neural components. For a known DAG,

    \[ X_i=f_{\theta_i}(X_{\operatorname{pa}(i)},U_i), \qquad U_i\sim p_i. \]

    The components can be conditional flows, decoder networks, autoregressive conditionals, implicit generators, or diffusion samplers. The graph and intervention rules supply causal semantics; the component family supplies expressivity and computation.

    With observed causally sufficient variables and known topological order, maximum likelihood can train the modular factorization

    \[ \log p_\theta(x)=\sum_i\log p_{\theta_i}(x_i\mid x_{\operatorname{pa}(i)}). \]

    This estimates observational mechanisms under invariance/modularity assumptions. It does not test that the supplied parent sets are causal, and highly flexible conditionals can fit several observationally equivalent graphs.

    The causal and neural layers should therefore remain separate in diagrams, code, and evaluation. A graph edge declares a structural parent relation; the box implementing its child mechanism declares a computational contract.

    This is a hybrid-composition connection, not an equivalence claim. Changing the mechanism family can change approximation error, likelihood access, sampling cost, and abduction. It leaves unresolved whether the graph, intervention, latent variables, or unit coupling are identified.

    Causal VAEs¤

    A causal VAE commonly combines an encoder \(q_\phi(z\mid x)\), a structured latent prior or neural SCM \(p_\theta(z)\), and a decoder \(p_\theta(x\mid z)\):

    \[ \log p_\theta(x) \ge \mathbb E_{q_\phi(z\mid x)}[\log p_\theta(x\mid z)] -D_{\mathrm{KL}}(q_\phi(z\mid x)\|p_\theta(z)). \]

    CausalVAE is an influential example using a neural structural causal layer and concept supervision. Its architecture is not a universal identifiability theorem. Posterior collapse, decoder misspecification, latent-coordinate ambiguity, and imperfect concept labels remain possible.

    For counterfactuals, the encoder must support abduction of unit-specific exogenous or latent noise. Encoding an image, changing a labeled latent, and decoding can be a useful edit, but it is a counterfactual only if the learned representation, structural intervention, and noise retention are causally justified.

    The VAE posterior introduces two uncertainty layers: uncertainty about the unit's latent/exogenous state and uncertainty about learned parameters/structure. Sampling a new \(z\) after intervention estimates a population regime; sampling from \(q(z\mid x)\) and reusing the causally appropriate components estimates a model-dependent counterfactual distribution. A point encoder can hide this distinction.

    Conditional flows and deep SCMs¤

    An invertible conditional mechanism

    \[ X_i=f_{\theta_i}(X_{\operatorname{pa}(i)},U_i) \]

    can provide exact abduction:

    \[ U_i=f_{\theta_i}^{-1}(X_{\operatorname{pa}(i)},X_i). \]

    This makes flows attractive for deep structural causal models. Exact invertibility of the neural map does not make the causal graph correct, and maximum likelihood can still choose observationally equivalent mechanisms. Discrete variables, non-invertible measurement, hidden confounding, and support mismatch require additional inference machinery.

    Exact abduction requires that \(u_i\mapsto f_i(pa_i,u_i)\) is invertible for fixed parents, with compatible dimensions/support and a correctly fitted mechanism. Causal Autoregressive Flows proves identifiability for specified affine/additive autoregressive-flow causal families and order settings. It does not mean every autoregressive flow ordering is a causal order.

    Adversarial and implicit mechanisms¤

    GAN-like components can model complex mechanism conditionals without tractable likelihoods. They support sampling under an assumed intervention but make abduction harder: many noise values may produce similar observations, and no encoder is supplied automatically. Cycle consistency or an auxiliary encoder does not prove recovery of the true exogenous state.

    Adversarial losses can also match interventional datasets across regimes. This is a shared estimator/objective pattern, not an algebraic equivalence between GANs and causal models. The discriminator distinguishes distributions; it does not determine whether the regime labels correspond to valid interventions.

    A causal GAN can condition on graph-consistent parent variables or match several regime distributions. Without an abductive encoder, individual counterfactuals remain set-valued or require posterior inference over noise. Cycle consistency can select one coupling among many observationally valid couplings without identifying the true unit-level one.

    Diffusion mechanisms¤

    A conditional diffusion model may parameterize

    \[ p_\theta(x_i\mid x_{\operatorname{pa}(i)}) \]

    or decode high-dimensional observations from causal latents. Under a specified SCM, replacing a parent mechanism and sampling descendants can approximate an intervention. However, classifier-free guidance, text prompting, or image editing are not automatically \(do\)-operations. Prompts usually condition a learned joint or steer a sampler; they need not sever incoming causal edges.

    Counterfactual diffusion additionally needs a coupling between factual and counterfactual randomness. Reusing an inversion trajectory is an algorithmic coupling. Calling it unit-level exogenous noise requires an SCM justification and stability analysis because inversion can be non-unique and solver-dependent.

    Diffusion Causal Models study counterfactual estimation under a known causal structure using diffusion/score machinery. Their image realism and minimality results are evidence for that construction and benchmark, not identification of counterfactual images from unrestricted observational data.

    Autoregressive Models, EBMs, and GFlowNets¤

    “Causal attention” in sequence models usually means a left-to-right information mask, not an SCM. An autoregressive factorization can follow a known causal order, but any joint density admits orderings that are predictive without being causal.

    An EBM can define an unnormalized mechanism or posterior over structures, while a GFlowNet can amortize sampling from such a reward. These components preserve uncertainty/diversity only relative to their energy or Bayesian score. Neither turns a misspecified graph score into causal truth.

    Worked modular generator¤

    Suppose the causal graph is \(A\to M\to Y\) with \(A\to Y\). Let

    \[ A=U_A, \quad M=f_M(A,U_M), \quad Y=f_Y(A,M,U_Y). \]

    Fit \(f_M\) with a conditional flow and \(f_Y\) with a conditional diffusion model. To sample \(p(Y\mid do(A=a))\):

    1. set \(A:=a\);
    2. sample \(U_M\) and generate \(M=f_M(a,U_M)\);
    3. sample from the diffusion mechanism for \(Y\) conditioned on \((a,M)\).

    To estimate a natural direct or indirect effect, additional cross-world identification assumptions are required; simply holding the generated mediator fixed is not automatically a valid mediation estimand. To produce an individual counterfactual, infer the factual \(U_M,U_Y\) and reuse them after changing \(A\).

    Hybrid systems and modularity¤

    Different mechanisms can use different generator families. This is hybrid composition, one of the curriculum's six relationship labels. A tractable flow for tabular variables, a categorical autoregressive model for events, and a diffusion decoder for images can coexist in one SCM. The scientific interface is the variable/mechanism boundary, not architectural uniformity.

    Chapter 3 retrieval Questions¤

    1. What makes a conditional flow useful for counterfactual abduction?
    2. Why is a left-to-right attention mask not a causal graph?
    3. Which component supplies causal meaning in a diffusion SCM?
    Complete solutions
    1. Invertibility in exogenous noise at fixed parents can recover the unit's disturbance exactly under the fitted mechanism.
    2. It encodes prediction order; observational factorization alone does not establish intervention semantics or causal direction.
    3. The SCM's variables, graph, structural mechanisms, exogenous coupling, and intervention rule—not diffusion training by itself.

    Independent practice and assessment map: deep structural generators¤

    Attempt each task before opening its hints. Score the causal and computational contracts separately; an expressive component can pass one and fail the other.

    1. P1.1 — Predict before computing: test the invertibility claim. Type: predict · Difficulty: foundation · Typical time: 7 min. A conditional flow fits every observational conditional exactly and is invertible in noise. Predict which abductive operation becomes available and which graph/counterfactual-identification claims remain unsupported.
    2. P1.2 — Execute a modular counterfactual. Type: derive · Difficulty: core · Typical time: 10 min. Let \(M=2A+U_M\) and \(Y=A+M+U_Y\). For factual \((A,M,Y)=(1,3,6)\), abduct both noises and compute the same-unit result under \(A:=0\). Compare with the fresh-noise population mean when both noises have mean zero.
    3. P1.3 — Select mechanisms by query. Type: compare · Difficulty: core · Typical time: 13 min. Design an SCM with tabular baseline, discrete treatment, continuous biomarker, and image outcome. Choose candidate families for each mechanism and state likelihood, sampling, and abduction consequences. No family choice may serve as the identification argument.
    4. P1.4 — Diagnose a guided edit. Type: diagnose · Difficulty: core · Typical time: 9 min. A diffusion editor receives “remove the disease” as text guidance and preserves the random seed. List the missing objects before its output can be called a patient counterfactual rather than a guided conditional sample.
    5. P1.5 — Decompose a causal-generation error. Type: diagnose · Difficulty: stretch · Typical time: 15 min. A generated intervention distribution disagrees with held-out trial data. Produce a diagnostic tree separating graph/identification error, mechanism estimation, approximate abduction, sampler error, and support shift.
    6. P1.6 — Transfer through hybrid composition. Type: design · Difficulty: stretch · Typical time: 18 min. Specify a causal generator using a conditional flow, categorical autoregressive mechanism, and diffusion measurement decoder. Define the module boundaries, intervention propagation order, factual-noise reuse policy, and validation for each interface.

    Feedback contract: deep structural generators¤

    Table CAUSAL.13. Feedback contract: deep structural generators — table 1
    Task Evidence of success
    P1.1 Exact local inversion is granted without promoting observational orientation or exogenous coupling to identified truth.
    P1.2 Both factual residuals are reused in causal order and distinguished from a population redraw.
    P1.3 Architecture choices follow variable type and query while identification remains upstream.
    P1.4 Variable semantics, graph, action, abduction, coupling, and validation are all required beyond guidance and seed reuse.
    P1.5 Statistical, causal, computational, and support failures occupy separate branches.
    P1.6 Mixed families communicate through declared variables/mechanisms rather than hidden architectural state.
    Graduated hints for P1
    Table CAUSAL.14. Feedback contract: deep structural generators — table 2
    Task Hint 1 — Direction Hint 2 — Setup
    P1.1 Invertibility answers an inference question conditional on the fitted mechanism. Ask whether another causal ordering could fit the same observational joint.
    P1.2 Solve mechanisms in topological order. \(U_M=M-2A\) and \(U_Y=Y-A-M\); reuse both after replacing \(A\).
    P1.3 Match local data types and inference needs. A categorical AR mechanism can model treatment; a flow can support exact biomarker abduction; an image decoder needs a factual posterior if unit edits are claimed.
    P1.4 A seed is not automatically an exogenous variable. Start with the measured causal state and the mechanism that the phrase is supposed to replace.
    P1.5 Check the target before checking the sampler. Branch in this order: query/graph, support, fitted mechanism, abduction, numerical generation.
    P1.6 Put the causal interface above each neural implementation. Name each \(X_i\), \(PA_i\), \(U_i\), and whether \(U_i\) is inferred or freshly sampled.

    Outcome-to-task alignment: deep structural generators¤

    Table CAUSAL.15. Outcome-to-task alignment: deep structural generators
    Learning objective Independently assessed by
    O1 — Separate semantics from parameterization P1.1, P1.3, P1.4
    O2 — Match mechanisms to computational contracts P1.1, P1.3, P1.6
    O3 — Execute modular interventions P1.2, P1.6
    O4 — Distinguish population and unit generation P1.2, P1.4, P1.6
    O5 — Diagnose neural pseudocausality P1.1, P1.4, P1.5
    O6 — Design hybrid causal generators P1.3, P1.6
    Complete solutions to P1
    1. P1.1. At fixed parents, the inverse flow recovers the fitted disturbance \(u_i=f_i^{-1}(pa_i,x_i)\), enabling exact local abduction within that model. Exact observational fit and inversion do not identify the parent set, causal direction, hidden-confounder structure, or true cross-world noise coupling; another SCM can fit the same joint. Check. Computational invertibility is conditional on a structural model, not proof of that model.
    2. P1.2. \(U_M=3-2(1)=1\) and \(U_Y=6-1-3=2\). Under \(A:=0\), reuse gives \(M_0=2(0)+1=1\) and \(Y_0=0+1+2=3\). Fresh mean-zero noises give \(E[M\mid do(A=0)]=0\) and \(E[Y\mid do(A=0)]=0\). Check. The first answer concerns the observed unit; the second averages new units from the intervention regime.
    3. P1.3. One defensible design uses a normalized tabular model for baseline, a categorical AR conditional for treatment, a conditional flow for biomarker when dimensions/support permit exact local inversion, and a latent or diffusion image mechanism conditioned on causal state. Unit image counterfactuals additionally need an encoder or posterior over measurement noise; an implicit decoder alone supplies no abduction. The graph and effect identification come from design/assumptions, not these family choices. Rubric. Other choices pass if their likelihood, sampling, and posterior tradeoffs are explicit.
    4. P1.4. The missing objects are a measured causal disease variable, its parents and descendants, a defined intervention that replaces its mechanism, an abducted factual patient state, a justified mapping between sampler randomness and exogenous variables, support for the requested action, and descendant/non-descendant validation against interventional evidence. Check. Text meaning and seed equality establish none of these automatically.
    5. P1.5. First verify the target population, intervention, graph, and identifying functional. Next stratify trial overlap/support. Then test each fitted invariant mechanism on its regime, followed by factual reconstruction/posterior calibration for abduction, and finally sampler convergence/discretization. Compare disagreement after each replacement with an oracle component. Rubric. Full credit does not begin by tuning the sampler while the estimand may be wrong.
    6. P1.6. For example, a flow implements continuous \(X_1=f_1(PA_1,U_1)\), a categorical AR model implements event \(X_2\mid PA_2\), and diffusion decodes measurement \(X_3\) from causal state. An intervention replaces the target module and regenerates only descendants in topological order. Population queries redraw all required disturbances; unit queries infer and retain factual disturbances outside the replaced mechanism. Validate local conditionals, inversion/posteriors, cross-module support, intervention responses, and end-to-end query calibration separately. Rubric. Hidden state passed directly between architectures without a causal variable contract fails.

    Chapter 3 synthesis¤

    Deep families make structural mechanisms expressive and scalable. They do not manufacture causal orientation, identification, or valid interventions. The central engineering requirement is modularity: intervene on one mechanism, preserve the appropriate exogenous information, and propagate only through descendants. Chapter 4 applies that machinery to shifts, counterfactuals, and world models.

    Is text guidance an intervention?

    Usually it is conditioning or sampler steering. It becomes an intervention only within a causal model where the guided variable and the replaced mechanism are explicitly defined.
