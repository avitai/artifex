    2. Statistical Manifolds and Natural Gradients¤

    Chapter 2 prerequisites and learning objectives¤

    Prerequisites. Scores, KL divergence, gradients, and constrained quadratic optimization.

    By the end, you should be able to:

    1. O1 — Construct statistical tangents: turn a parameter direction into a zero-mean tangent score under regularity.
    2. O2 — Derive Fisher geometry: obtain the local quadratic form from score covariance or a local KL expansion.
    3. O3 — Compute natural directions: solve on an identifiable tangent space and interpret a pseudoinverse rather than hiding singularity.
    4. O4 — Verify coordinate covariance: transform gradients, Fishers, and tangent directions under an invertible reparameterization.
    5. O5 — Bound invariance claims: separate the exact infinitesimal vector from damping, finite steps, factorization, clipping, and optimizer state.
    6. O6 — Transfer to a finite law: compute and validate a natural movement for a categorical or other small distribution.

    A tangent vector changes a law¤

    Consider a differentiable family

    \[ \mathcal M=\{p_\theta:\theta\in\Theta\subseteq\mathbb R^d\}. \]

    A parameter-space direction \(v\) induces the tangent score

    \[ u_v(x)=v^\top\nabla_\theta\log p_\theta(x). \]

    For a regular family with parameter-independent support, \(\mathbb E_{p_\theta}[u_v(X)]=0\). The Fisher--Rao inner product between two tangent directions is

    \[ g_\theta(v,w) =\mathbb E_{p_\theta}[u_v(X)u_w(X)] =v^\top F(\theta)w. \]

    This construction measures first-order changes in the represented probability law. It is not a metric on the semantic quality of samples, the Euclidean distance between neural weights, or the geometry of individual latent codes.

    From a local discrepancy to a natural direction¤

    Under the regularity conditions stated in the optimization foundation,

    \[ D_{\mathrm{KL}}(p_\theta\|p_{\theta+\Delta}) =\frac12\Delta^\top F(\theta)\Delta +o(\lVert\Delta\rVert^2). \]

    For a scalar objective \(J(\theta)\) with Euclidean coordinate gradient \(g=\nabla_\theta J\), the infinitesimal steepest descent vector under this metric solves

    \[ F(\theta)v_{\mathrm{nat}}=g, \qquad \dot\theta=-v_{\mathrm{nat}}. \]

    If \(F\) is singular, the equation is interpreted on the identifiable tangent space. The Moore--Penrose solution \(F^\dagger g\) is meaningful only when the objective gradient is compatible with that quotient geometry. A numerical inverse cannot manufacture statistical identifiability.

    What invariance does and does not say¤

    Let \(\theta=h(\psi)\) be a smooth locally invertible coordinate change with Jacobian \(J_h\). Then

    \[ F_\psi=J_h^\top F_\theta J_h, \qquad \nabla_\psi J=J_h^\top\nabla_\theta J. \]

    The exact infinitesimal natural vector transforms as a tangent vector, so both coordinates describe the same first-order motion of the law. This statement does not make the following objects invariant automatically:

    • a finite Euler step;
    • damping by \(\lambda I\) in whichever coordinates happen to be used;
    • diagonal, block-diagonal, Kronecker, or low-rank approximations;
    • momentum or adaptive state stored in parameter coordinates;
    • clipping and per-parameter learning-rate schedules; or
    • a non-invertible map between overparameterized descriptions.

    Martens gives a careful account of these boundaries. The original efficiency argument is due to Amari.

    Worked categorical law: logits versus probabilities¤

    Let a three-category model have probabilities

    \[ p=(0.8,0.1,0.1) \]

    and let the target frequencies be \(r=(0.6,0.2,0.2)\). For softmax logits \(\theta\), the cross-entropy gradient is

    \[ g_\theta=p-r=(0.2,-0.1,-0.1), \]

    and the Fisher matrix is

    \[ F_\theta=\operatorname{diag}(p)-pp^\top. \]

    It is singular because adding one constant to every logit does not change \(p\). Any natural logit direction satisfying \(F_\theta v=g_\theta\) is defined only up to that null direction. Nevertheless, the induced probability motion is unique:

    \[ \dot p=-F_\theta v=-(p-r). \]

    With a small time step \(0.1\), the first-order update is \(p^+=(0.78,0.11,0.11)\). The calculation shows what invariance preserves: the motion of the categorical law, not a unique motion of redundant logits.

    Metric, divergence, and optimizer are three objects

    KL supplies the local Fisher metric under regularity. A finite natural-gradient optimizer additionally chooses a solve, damping, step rule, stochastic estimator, and state update. Two implementations that cite the same metric can therefore follow measurably different trajectories.

    Independent practice and assessment map: statistical manifolds¤

    Work in exact finite distributions before invoking a neural approximation. Prediction tasks ask for the law movement first; coordinate algebra comes second.

    1. P1.1 — Derive Bernoulli geometry. Type: derive · Difficulty: core · Typical time: 12 min. For \(X\sim\operatorname{Bernoulli}(q)\), derive the score and Fisher in probability coordinate \(q\). For cross-entropy to target frequency \(r\), derive the natural descent flow of \(q\).
    2. P1.2 — Predict before computing: the categorical null direction. Type: predict · Difficulty: foundation · Typical time: 8 min. Before multiplying matrices, predict what a common shift of all softmax logits does to the represented law. Then prove that the all-ones vector is in the nullspace of \(F=\operatorname{diag}(p)-pp^\top\).
    3. P1.3 — Verify coordinate covariance. Type: derive · Difficulty: core · Typical time: 13 min. Re-express P1.1 using logit \(\eta=\log(q/(1-q))\). Show that the natural directions in \(q\) and \(\eta\) induce the same infinitesimal motion of the Bernoulli law.
    4. P1.4 — Expose damping dependence numerically. Type: derive · Difficulty: stretch · Typical time: 12 min. Let \(J(q)=\tfrac12(q-r)^2\), \(q=0.5\), \(r=0.25\), and add scalar damping \(\lambda=1\) separately in \(q\) and logit coordinates. Compute each damped direction and its induced \(q\)-motion. Are they invariant?
    5. P1.5 — Validate a finite categorical step. Type: diagnose · Difficulty: core · Typical time: 11 min. Starting from \(p=(0.8,0.1,0.1)\) toward \(r=(0.6,0.2,0.2)\), compare the first-order law step \(p+0.1(r-p)\) with a finite logit Euler step along the minimum-norm natural direction. Specify two quantities that should be reported rather than assuming equality.
    6. P1.6 — Transfer to a Poisson family. Type: transfer · Difficulty: stretch · Typical time: 16 min. For \(X\sim\operatorname{Poisson}(\lambda)\), derive the Fisher in coordinates \(\lambda\) and \(\eta=\log\lambda\). For negative log likelihood of one observation \(x\), derive the natural descent flow of the mean \(\lambda\) and identify the regularity boundary at \(\lambda=0\).

    P1.1 The score is centered, Fisher is positive on the interior, and natural flow moves the law toward \(r\).
    P1.2 Parameter redundancy is connected to a zero law tangent, not treated as numerical bad luck.

    Complete solutions to P1
    1. P1.1. The score is \(s_q(X)=X/q-(1-X)/(1-q)=(X-q)/[q(1-q)]\), whose expectation is zero. Its second moment is \(F_q=1/[q(1-q)]\). Cross-entropy has \(\partial_qJ=(q-r)/[q(1-q)]\), so the natural direction is \(F_q^{-1}\partial_qJ=q-r\) and descent gives \(\dot q=r-q\). Check. The result stays inside the tangent space only for interior \(q\); a finite integrator still needs a boundary-safe convention.
    2. P1.2. Adding \(c\mathbf1\) multiplies numerator and denominator of softmax by the same factor, so \(p\) is unchanged. Since \(p^\top\mathbf1=1\), \(F\mathbf1=\operatorname{diag}(p)\mathbf1-pp^\top\mathbf1=p-p=0\). Check. The singular direction is statistically invisible.
    3. P1.3. In logit coordinates, \(s_\eta=X-q\), \(F_\eta=q(1-q)\), and \(\partial_\eta J=q-r\). The natural descent is \(\dot\eta=-(q-r)/[q(1-q)]\). Multiplying by \(dq/d\eta=q(1-q)\) gives \(\dot q=r-q\), exactly the probability-coordinate tangent. Check. The coordinate components differ while the law tangent agrees.
    4. P1.4. In \(q\), the damped solve gives \(d_q=g_q/(F_q+1)=0.25/5=0.05\). In \(\eta\), \(d_\eta=0.0625/(0.25+1)=0.05\), but its induced \(q\)-direction is \((dq/d\eta)d_\eta=0.25(0.05)=0.0125\). Thus the same scalar damping value yields four-times different first-order law movement. Check. Equal coordinate numbers are not invariant tensors.
    5. P1.5. The first-order probability step is \((0.78,0.11,0.11)\). A minimum-norm logit solution \(v\) of \(Fv=p-r\) followed by \(\theta^+=\theta-0.1v\) and softmax generally differs by second-order terms. Report the realized \(D_{\mathrm{KL}}(p\|p^+)\) and objective change, and repeat over decreasing step sizes to verify first-order convergence. Rubric. Exact agreement at finite step is not required or expected.
    6. P1.6. In mean coordinate, \(s_\lambda=X/\lambda-1\) and \(F_\lambda=1/\lambda\). For one observation, \(J=\lambda-x\log\lambda+\text{const}\) and \(g_\lambda=1-x/\lambda=(\lambda-x)/\lambda\), so natural descent gives \(\dot\lambda=x-\lambda\). With \(\eta=\log\lambda\), \(s_\eta=X-\lambda\), \(F_\eta=\lambda\), and the induced mean motion is again \(x-\lambda\). At \(\lambda=0\) the regular interior coordinate and fixed-support score calculation break down. Check. The boundary is a model condition, not cured by matrix inversion.

    Chapter 2 synthesis and retrieval practice¤

    A statistical tangent is a first-order change of law. Fisher--Rao geometry identifies parameter directions only up to distributionally invisible motion, and its invariance is exact for the underlying infinitesimal vector—not for every finite approximation.

    Retrieval questions
    1. Why is the categorical Fisher singular in logit coordinates?
    2. What object remains invariant under a smooth coordinate change?
    3. Why can \(F+\lambda I\) weaken invariance?

    Solutions. (1) A common logit shift leaves the categorical law unchanged. (2) The exact infinitesimal tangent motion of the probability law. (3) The identity matrix is coordinate dependent, so adding it changes the metric in a parameterization-specific way.

    Bridge. Chapter 3 uses exponential-family dual coordinates to turn several apparently second-order updates into simple moment updates.
