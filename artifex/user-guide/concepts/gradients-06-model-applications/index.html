    6. Likelihood, Latent, and Energy Model Applications¤

    Chapter 6 prerequisites and learning objectives¤

    Prerequisites. Autoregressive factorization, changes of variables, VAEs, and energy-based maximum likelihood.

    By the end, you should be able to:

    1. O1 — Decompose autoregressive Fisher: prove conditional score orthogonality while retaining shared-parameter coupling.
    2. O2 — Audit the context law: distinguish joint-model curvature from teacher-forced data-context curvature.
    3. O3 — Locate flow geometry: separate exact pointwise density evaluation, model expectation, weight reparameterization, and sample-coordinate change.
    4. O4 — Separate VAE Fishers: identify generative-joint, observed-marginal, variational-conditional, and declared GGN metrics.
    5. O5 — Couple EBM errors correctly: trace negative-phase sampling error into both the likelihood gradient and Fisher estimate.
    6. O6 — Transfer by query: determine when structured tractability changes the expectation bottleneck without changing the definition of geometry.

    Autoregressive models: a conditional Fisher sum¤

    For

    \[ p_\theta(x_{1:T})=\prod_{t=1}^T p_\theta(x_t\mid x_{<t}), \]

    write the joint score as \(S=\sum_t s_t\), where \(s_t=\nabla_\theta\log p_\theta(x_t\mid x_{<t})\). Under regular conditional support,

    \[ \mathbb E[s_t\mid X_{<t}]=0. \]

    For \(u<t\), \(s_u\) is measurable with respect to the later history, so \(\mathbb E[s_us_t^\top]=0\). Therefore

    \[ F_{\mathrm{AR}}(\theta) =\sum_{t=1}^T \mathbb E_{X_{<t}\sim p_\theta} \left[F_t(\theta;X_{<t})\right]. \]

    The factorization removes cross-time score covariances in the exact expectation; it does not make the shared-parameter Fisher block diagonal. Every token can still update the same embedding and Transformer weights.

    For teacher-forced training, contexts are commonly drawn from the data rather than the model. The resulting conditional curvature can be useful, but its context distribution differs from the exact joint model Fisher. Report which one is estimated.

    Normalizing flows: exact density does not imply a cheap Fisher¤

    An invertible flow makes \(\log p_\theta(x)\) tractable through a base log density and a log-Jacobian term. This permits exact pointwise scores with respect to parameters, but the model Fisher still averages their outer products under \(X\sim p_\theta\). Sampling is direct, so Monte Carlo estimation is conceptually clean; the matrix remains enormous and can be ill-conditioned near redundant transformations.

    Reparameterizing the weights of the same flow and changing the sample coordinates through the flow are different operations. Fisher invariance addresses the first. The change-of-variables theorem addresses the second.

    VAEs: encoder and decoder geometries must be separated¤

    The VAE ELBO

    \[ \mathcal L(\theta,\phi) =\mathbb E_{p_D(x)q_\phi(z\mid x)} [\log p_\theta(x,z)-\log q_\phi(z\mid x)] \]

    couples a generative-model parameter \(\theta\) and an inference parameter \(\phi\). Possible metrics include:

    • the joint-model Fisher of \(p_\theta(x,z)\);
    • the observed-model Fisher of \(p_\theta(x)\), which usually requires posterior expectations;
    • the conditional variational Fisher of \(q_\phi(z\mid x)\) averaged over data;
    • a generalized Gauss--Newton approximation for a declared decoder likelihood; and
    • block or Kronecker approximations in the shared networks.

    These metrics answer different questions. In particular, preconditioning the encoder ELBO gradient by \(F_\phi^{(q)}\) is a natural step for the amortized posterior family, not for the marginal data model.

    EBMs: Fisher estimation inherits the negative-phase problem¤

    For an exponential-family EBM

    \[ p_\theta(x)=\exp\{\theta^\top T(x)-A(\theta)\}, \]

    the likelihood gradient and Fisher are

    \[ \nabla_\theta\ell =\mathbb E_{p_D}[T(X)]-\mathbb E_{p_\theta}[T(X)], \qquad F(\theta)=\operatorname{Cov}_{p_\theta}[T(X)]. \]

    Both require model moments. If an MCMC chain has not mixed, it can bias both the gradient and the curvature estimate, and solving the biased system more accurately does not restore maximum-likelihood learning. For nonlinear energies, the parameter score is the centered energy derivative,

    \[ \nabla_\theta\log p_\theta(x) =-\nabla_\theta E_\theta(x) +\mathbb E_{p_\theta}[\nabla_\theta E_\theta(X)]. \]

    Probabilistic circuits or other tractable structured models may compute some required expectations exactly. Their natural-gradient advantage is therefore a query property of the circuit, not a consequence of neural depth.

    Worked conditional-score cancellation¤

    For a two-step AR model, \(S=s_1+s_2\). The cross term is

    \[ \mathbb E[s_1s_2^\top] =\mathbb E\!\left[ s_1\,\mathbb E[s_2^\top\mid X_1] \right] =0. \]

    Thus \(F=\mathbb E[s_1s_1^\top]+\mathbb E[s_2s_2^\top]\). If both scores depend on the same scalar network weight, however, these are two contributions to one scalar Fisher—not two independent optimizer blocks. Orthogonality of random score increments is not architectural parameter separation.

    Independent practice and assessment map: family-specific Fisher queries¤

    Start each task by naming the normalized family and the law under which score products are averaged. Architectural tractability is evaluated only afterward.

    1. P1.1 — Prove conditional-score cancellation. Type: derive · Difficulty: core · Typical time: 11 min. For a three-step autoregressive model, prove \(\mathbb E[s_us_t^\top]=0\) for \(u<t\). State why this does not make the neural parameter Fisher block diagonal.
    2. P1.2 — Predict before computing: compare context laws. Type: derive · Difficulty: core · Typical time: 9 min. A conditional model has Fisher \(F_0=1\) in context zero and \(F_1=4\) in context one. Predict which averaging law gives larger curvature, then compute both averages and identify each object.
    3. P1.3 — Locate geometry in a flow. Type: derive · Difficulty: foundation · Typical time: 10 min. For location flow \(X=\theta+Z\), \(Z\sim\mathcal N(0,1)\), derive the parameter score and Fisher. Distinguish reparameterizing \(\theta\) from changing sample coordinates through the flow.
    4. P1.4 — Choose among VAE metrics. Type: compare · Difficulty: core · Typical time: 12 min. For an encoder-only update, a decoder-only update, and a claim about the observed data law, name the most directly relevant conditional, joint/GGN, or marginal Fisher and the expectation each requires.
    5. P1.5 — Quantify an EBM sampling failure. Type: diagnose · Difficulty: stretch · Typical time: 11 min. In a two-state EBM with \(T(X)=X\), the data mean is \(0.6\) and the true model mean is \(0.8\). A stale negative-phase chain has mean \(0.5\). Compute the true and stale likelihood gradients and the true and stale Bernoulli covariance estimates.
    6. P1.6 — Transfer to a tractable circuit. Type: design · Difficulty: stretch · Typical time: 17 min. A probabilistic circuit can compute its partition function and selected sufficient-statistic moments exactly. Specify which gradient/Fisher queries become exact, which matrix or solve problems remain, and how to verify the advantage against Monte Carlo neural baselines.

    Complete solutions to P1
    1. P1.1. For \(u<t\), \(s_u\) is a function of \(X_{\le u}\) and hence measurable given \(X_{<t}\). Therefore \(\mathbb E[s_us_t^\top]=\mathbb E[s_u\mathbb E[s_t^\top\mid X_{<t}]]=0\). The same shared network parameters can occur in every \(s_t\), so the surviving conditional Fisher contributions sum into the same parameter matrix. Check. Orthogonal random increments do not imply disjoint parameter blocks.
    2. P1.2. The model-history Fisher is \(0.75(1)+0.25(4)=1.75\). The teacher-forced conditional curvature is \(0.25(1)+0.75(4)=3.25\). Both can be useful, but only the first uses contexts from the joint model distribution specified in the exact AR Fisher. Check. Reusing identical conditional code does not equalize the outer context law.
    3. P1.3. \(\partial_\theta\log p_\theta(x)=x-\theta=Z\), so \(F=\mathbb E[Z^2]=1\). Replacing \(\theta\) by a smooth invertible parameter coordinate transforms this same family and its Fisher. The map \(Z\mapsto X=\theta+Z\) instead changes sample coordinates and supplies density/sample reparameterization; it is not a weight-coordinate invariance statement. Check. Direct sampling makes the expectation easy, not conceptually absent.
    4. P1.4. Encoder change is directly measured by the conditional Fisher of \(q_\phi(z\mid x)\) averaged over a declared \(x\) law. Decoder change can use the joint-model Fisher of \(p_\theta(x,z)\) or a declared likelihood GGN under data/posterior samples. A marginal-data-law claim requires the observed Fisher of \(p_\theta(x)\), whose score generally averages complete-data scores under \(p_\theta(z\mid x)\). Rubric. The answer states parameter block and averaging distribution for each.
    5. P1.5. The true maximum-likelihood gradient is \(0.6-0.8=-0.2\); the stale-chain estimate is \(0.6-0.5=0.1\), even reversing sign. The true Fisher is \(0.8(0.2)=0.16\); treating the stale chain as Bernoulli mean \(0.5\) gives \(0.25\). Check. A more accurate solve with \(0.25\) would precondition the wrong gradient with the wrong curvature.
    6. P1.6. Exact normalization and moments can make likelihood gradients, score centering, Fisher entries, or FVP queries exact when the circuit supports those marginals. The parameter matrix may still be large, singular, or expensive to solve, so matrix-free products, structured blocks, damping, and trust checks remain. Compare exact values with Monte Carlo bias/variance, wall-clock, memory, solve accuracy, and held-out likelihood/sample queries at matched parameter points and budgets. Rubric. The advantage is stated per supported query, not attributed generically to “tractability.”

    Chapter 6 synthesis and retrieval practice¤

    Normalized likelihoods identify a Fisher cleanly, but tractability varies. Autoregression supplies a conditional decomposition, flows supply direct model samples, VAEs contain several candidate statistical families, and EBMs inherit the difficulty of equilibrium model expectations.

    Retrieval questions
    1. Why do exact AR cross-time score terms vanish?
    2. Why is the encoder Fisher not the VAE data-model Fisher?
    3. What two EBM quantities can be biased by poor model samples?

    Solutions. (1) Each conditional score has zero expectation given its history. (2) It measures changes in \(q_\phi(z\mid x)\), not changes in \(p_\theta(x)\). (3) The likelihood negative phase and the model-Fisher covariance.

    Bridge. Implicit generators and path models make the choice of law even more consequential: an ambient endpoint density may be absent, while a surrogate conditional or transport discrepancy remains available.
