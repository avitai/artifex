    6. Evaluation, Deployment, and Research Directions¤

    Capstone objectives¤

    By the end, you should be able to:

    1. select a PC from a declared query workload;
    2. benchmark inference as a compiled system;
    3. distinguish exact arithmetic from calibration, robustness, causality, privacy, and correctness of the learned distribution;
    4. trace every research claim to its evidence maturity; and
    5. design an end-to-end study that tests both model quality and tractability.

    When a Probabilistic Circuit Is a Strong Choice¤

    Prefer a PC when the application repeatedly needs exact missing-data marginals, arbitrary evidence queries, conditionals, logical constraints, a tractable tabular density whose calibration is separately validated, or expectations that match a compatible circuit structure. They are especially compelling when query latency matters more than unconstrained perceptual sample quality.

    Do not choose a PC solely because “exact inference” sounds safer. A small misspecified circuit answers the wrong model exactly. High-dimensional raw perception may require large circuits, hybrid features, or a different family.

    Exactness also does not imply calibrated uncertainty or OOD robustness. Probabilistic Circuits That Know What They Don't Know demonstrates overconfidence and distribution-shift failures in standard PCs, then studies tractable uncertainty estimation. The UAI 2023 result is evidence that uncertainty must be evaluated, not a guarantee that its proposed estimator repairs every shift.

    Evaluation Matrix¤

    Table PC.2. Evaluation Matrix
    Claim Evidence to report

    Production Checklist¤

    1. Validate nonnegative normalized sum weights after serialization and quantization.
    2. Test smoothness, decomposability, determinism, and vtree conformity as executable contracts.
    3. Evaluate in log space or with scaling for deep circuits.
    4. Guard conditional queries when evidence probability is zero or extremely small.
    5. Version preprocessing, leaf support, variable ordering, and missing-value conventions.
    6. Benchmark the actual query mix; likelihood evaluation and conditional sampling have different costs.
    7. Trace hybrid feature transformations so “exact” is attached to the correct variable.

    Benchmark the compiled artifact, not the family name¤

    Circuit latency depends on more than node count. Record edges, active edges under determinism/evidence, leaf cost, tensor shapes, sparsity layout, batch size, dtype, compilation time, peak memory, device, and whether values are in probability or log space. Dense tensorization can execute nominally unused edges; sparse kernels can lose accelerator efficiency; a smaller irregular circuit can be slower than a larger regular one.

    Separate at least four workloads:

    Table PC.3. Benchmark the compiled artifact, not the family name
    Workload Dominant operations Additional report
    Complete likelihood one upward point-evaluation pass examples/second and per-example latency
    Arbitrary marginal batch upward passes with mask-dependent leaf values mask distribution, cache reuse, number of distinct masks
    Conditional sampling evidence pass plus repeated top-down traces samples per evidence case, leaf conditional cost
    Learning upward/downward passes and parameter synchronization examples/second, optimizer, structure-search and compile cost

    Amortized compilation may be appropriate for repeated queries but misleading for a one-off query. Report cold and warm paths when both matter.

    A query-first selection procedure¤

    1. Write the required queries and approximation tolerance.
    2. Choose the weakest structural properties that guarantee them.
    3. Select leaf interfaces and state-space semantics.
    4. estimate or measure circuit size under those restrictions;
    5. compare against AR, flow, VAE, graphical-model, or approximate-inference baselines on the same workload; and
    6. reject the circuit route if compilation/learning size overwhelms its query advantage.

    This avoids demanding determinism or structured compatibility “just in case,” which can pay an expressivity cost without serving a real query.

    Research Directions¤

    Research directions include structure restructuring, data partitioning at scale, soft structure induction, minibatch EM with distribution-aware regularization, flow/circuit hybrids, circuit distillation, causal compositions, and circuits over structured objects. Exactness claims remain property-specific. Scaling a model while preserving smoothness/decomposability is not the same as making MAP, arbitrary circuit products, or causal effects tractable.

    The primary-source audit covers peer-reviewed work on soft learning, restructuring, data-partition scaling, and anemone parameter learning. Search results without clear archival acceptance remain excluded or explicitly labeled; the page metadata records the audit date.

    Compositional probabilistic and causal inference demonstrates how marginal determinism and structural formulations expand causal queries. Such results do not make observational PCs causal by default; the causal graph/mechanisms and identifiability assumptions remain necessary.

    Together, these results address circuit compatibility, effective capacity, distributed learning, representation learning, and parameter optimization. They do not support a narrative that PCs replaced unrestricted neural generators. The distinctive research target is a Pareto trade-off among circuit size, learned fit, query set, numerical/system cost, and evidence quality.

    Research Exercises¤

    1. Implement the Chapter 3 circuit and verify all four joint probabilities sum to one.
    2. Compute \(p(Y=1\mid X=0)\) both by enumeration and circuit marginalization.
    3. Break decomposability by multiplying two children that both contain \(X\); show why setting \(X\) leaves to one no longer performs correct marginalization.
    4. Compare hard and soft responsibilities for an overlapping two-component mixture.
    5. Design a circuit/flow hybrid and state precisely which likelihood and marginal queries remain exact.
    Exercise sketches
    1. Evaluate each assignment and sum; normalized leaves and mixture weights yield one.
    2. Compute \(p(X=0,Y=1)/p(X=0)\); both procedures must agree.
    3. The shared variable is integrated twice, so distributivity no longer represents one marginal integral.
    4. Hard routing discards uncertainty; soft EM assigns fractional counts to both components.
    5. Exactness requires normalized invertible flow components and circuit decomposability; cross-circuit operations need additional compatibility.

    Cumulative worked study: clinical records with arbitrary missingness¤

    Suppose a hospital wants a joint model of demographics, laboratory values, diagnoses, and outcomes for imputation and cohort queries.

    1. Semantics: declare categorical/counting and continuous/Lebesgue leaves, support transforms, units, and whether missingness indicators are modeled variables or query masks.
    2. Query workload: list joint likelihood, \(p(\text{outcome}\mid e)\), arbitrary lab marginals, conditional sampling, and cohort expectations. Do not request MAP, entropy, or causal effects without checking their extra properties.
    3. Structure: choose smooth/decomposable structure; require determinism only if its query benefit justifies size; choose a shared vtree when circuit products or policy/utility expectations are required.
    4. Learning: split patients rather than rows to avoid leakage; compare EM/gradient/anemone under fixed structure; nest structure and hyperparameter selection inside validation; record zero-flow handling.
    5. Evaluation: report held-out NLL in declared units, calibration, mask-pattern performance, multiple-imputation coverage, subgroup error, OOD shifts, circuit/leaf size, and query latency distributions.
    6. Safety boundary: observational conditionals are not treatment effects. Privacy needs an explicit mechanism/threat model. Exact inference cannot compensate for selection bias, unmeasured confounding, or invalid missingness assumptions.

    This study demonstrates the book's central rule: first declare variables and queries, then choose properties and leaves, then learn and benchmark the complete compiled artifact.

    Independent practice and assessment map: circuit evaluation and research¤

    Choose a domain different from the worked clinical study. Bind the probability object, compiled representation, query workload, evidence claim, and deployment measurement in one falsifiable decision record.

    P1 — Defend an end-to-end probabilistic-circuit system¤

    1. P1.1 — Predict before computing. Classify each statement as an algebraic guarantee, assumption-bounded guarantee, empirical claim, hybrid composition, or false inference: exact marginal arithmetic implies calibrated uncertainty; linear time in edges implies low latency; determinism is always worth its size cost; a compiled observational circuit identifies treatment effects; tensorization changes the modeled law; federated learning supplies privacy without a threat model; and a lower held-out NLL proves better arbitrary-missingness decisions. State the extra evidence required to strengthen each statement.
    2. P1.2 — Query-first system design. For an industrial sensor fleet with irregular missing channels and repeated alarm queries, declare variables, supports, measures, missingness semantics, exact and approximate queries, tolerance, circuit properties, vtree or region structure, leaf interfaces, learning method, and zero-evidence policy. Compare the PC with at least two AR, flow, VAE, graphical-model, or imputation baselines on the same query workload and give a falsifiable selection rule.
    3. P1.3 — Compiled-artifact benchmark. Design cold and warm benchmarks for complete likelihood, mask-dependent marginals, conditional sampling, and learning. Record nodes, edges, active edges, leaf cost, tensor shapes, sparsity, batch/mask distribution, dtype, compilation time, cache reuse, samples per evidence case, peak memory, device, latency quantiles, throughput, and energy or total compute. Include a finite enumerated circuit oracle and a matched dense/sparse implementation comparison.
    4. P1.4 — Research evidence audit. Select three checkpoints from restructuring, data-partition scaling, approximation hardness, representation learning, parameter learning, or PC–LLM expressivity. Record exact theorem/claim, assumptions, circuit class, query, size measure, data/split, optimization/search budget, hardware, uncertainty, publication status/version, and independent reproduction. Rewrite each headline as the strongest protocol-bounded statement the record supports.
    5. P1.5 — Transfer and safety boundary. Transfer the sensor system to either privacy-sensitive genomic cohorts or variable-size molecular configurations. Identify every change in support, measure, symmetry, missingness/selection mechanism, constraint logic, leaf interface, vtree, query, metric, baseline, privacy/causal assumption, and compiled-system cost. Route each unsupported exactness, calibration, privacy, fairness, or causal claim to a proof, executable oracle, controlled study, explicit limitation, or rejection.

    Primary Reading Trail¤

    • Choi, Vergari, and Van den Broeck, Probabilistic Circuits: A Unifying Framework — semantics, queries, and structural properties.
    • Poon and Domingos, Sum-Product Networks — foundational deep sum/product architecture.
    • Peharz et al., Einsum Networks — scalable tensorized circuits.
    • Vergari et al., A Compositional Atlas of Tractable Circuit Operations — property-driven closure and hardness for transformations and information-theoretic queries.

    • Dang, Vergari, and Van den Broeck, Strudel — structured-decomposable learning.
    • Sidheekh, Kersting, and Natarajan, Probabilistic Flow Circuits — flow/circuit composition.

    • Seng et al., Scaling Probabilistic Circuits via Data Partitioning — UAI 2025 distributed/federated learning construction.

    Final Synthesis¤

    The density–latent–energy relationship guide compares circuit normalization and exact marginalization with AR factorization, invertible flows, and monolithic energy networks.

    Probabilistic circuits trade unrestricted architecture for an algebra in which selected queries compile to exact dynamic programs. Leaves model local distributions, sums mix alternatives, products factor disjoint scopes, and graph sharing reuses computation. Smoothness, decomposability, determinism, and structured decomposability are separate tools with separate guarantees. Modern neural, flow, relational, and distilled circuits expand local capacity, but the central research discipline remains unchanged: name the query, state the structural properties, and measure the circuit size required to keep it tractable.

    Cumulative retrieval practice: the complete circuit volume
    1. Prove normalization of a smooth decomposable circuit by induction.
    2. Why is complexity linear in circuit size not the same as tractability in the original problem description?
    3. Which property justifies ordinary marginal recursion, and which additional property can justify max-product MPE?
    4. Derive a normalized sum-edge responsibility from an upward value and downward derivative.
    5. Give two ways a hybrid can retain complete density but lose arbitrary marginals.
    6. What does exact inference fail to guarantee about a deployed clinical model?

    Solutions. (1) Leaves integrate to one; a decomposable product's integral factors into child integrals; a smooth normalized sum integrates to the weighted sum of ones. (2) compilation or structural restrictions may produce an exponentially large circuit. (3) Smoothness plus decomposability supports marginals; suitable determinism plus leaf maximization supports MPE. (4) Divide \((\partial C/\partial v_n)w_{nc}v_c\) by the root value \(C(x)\). (5) A non-invertible neural encoder changes the modeled variable; a full-scope flow leaf may not expose subset marginals. (6) Calibration, causal validity, privacy, robustness, fairness, data representativeness, numerical safety, or that the learned distribution is correct.

    Source-tracing checklist¤

    For every technical or research claim, record the circuit class; leaf family and base measure; smoothness, decomposability, determinism, and compatibility assumptions; query; complexity in nodes/edges and leaf cost; preprocessing; dataset/split/missingness; parameter and effective-edge counts; optimizer/structure search; hardware and compilation; metric and uncertainty; publication status/version; and independent reproduction. If a paper does not report an item, mark it unreported rather than inferring it.
