    Loss Function and Training¤

    The VAE loss is derived directly from the negative ELBO:

    \[ \mathcal{L}(\theta, \phi; x) = -\mathbb{E}_{q_\phi(z|x)}[\log p_\theta(x|z)] + D_{\text{KL}}(q_\phi(z|x) \| p(z)) \]

    Loss Function and Training: Prerequisites and learning objectives¤

    This chapter assumes the ELBO and Gaussian reparameterization from Chapter 1 plus basic automatic differentiation. By the end, you should be able to:

    1. implement a one-sample Monte Carlo negative ELBO with explicit RNG ownership;
    2. derive the diagonal-Gaussian KL and validate its zero case;
    3. keep batch, observation-dimension, and latent-dimension reductions consistent;
    4. diagnose posterior collapse without relying on one arbitrary KL range;
    5. distinguish optimization failure, inference-family limitation, and model misspecification; and
    6. state exactly what ELBO optimization does and does not establish about maximum likelihood.

    Practical Implementation¤

    For Gaussian encoder \(q_\phi(z|x) = \mathcal{N}(\mu, \sigma^2\mathbf{I})\) and standard normal prior \(p(z) = \mathcal{N}(0, \mathbf{I})\):

    Negative reconstruction log-likelihood for an isotropic Gaussian decoder \(p_\theta(x\mid z)=\mathcal N(\mu_\theta(z),\sigma_x^2I)\) with fixed, declared \(\sigma_x^2\):

    \[ -\log p_\theta(x\mid z) =\frac{1}{2\sigma_x^2}\|x-\mu_\theta(z)\|_2^2 +\frac{D}{2}\log(2\pi\sigma_x^2). \]

    MSE is therefore proportional to this term only after fixing the observation variance and reduction convention. Omitting the scale or switching between sum and mean silently changes the relative weight of reconstruction and KL.

    KL Divergence (closed-form for Gaussians; see Kingma & Welling, 2014, Appendix B):

    \[ \mathcal{L}_{\text{KL}} = -\frac{1}{2} \sum_{j=1}^{d} (1 + \log \sigma_j^2 - \mu_j^2 - \sigma_j^2) \]

    Total Loss:

    \[ \mathcal{L}_{\text{total}} = \mathcal{L}_{\text{recon}} + \mathcal{L}_{\text{KL}} \]

    Training Algorithm¤

    import jax
import jax.numpy as jnp
from flax import nnx
import optax

# Create optimizer (wrt=nnx.Param required in NNX 0.11.0+)
optimizer = nnx.Optimizer(vae, optax.adam(1e-3), wrt=nnx.Param)
rng_key = jax.random.key(0)

for epoch in range(num_epochs):
    for batch in dataloader:
        rng_key, sample_key = jax.random.split(rng_key)

        def loss_fn(vae):
            # Forward pass
            mu, log_var = vae.encoder(batch)

            # Reparameterization trick
            epsilon = jax.random.normal(sample_key, mu.shape)
            z = mu + jnp.exp(0.5 * log_var) * epsilon

            # Decode
            x_recon = vae.decoder(z)

            # ELBO terms — use a *consistent reduction*: sum over non-batch
            # axes (pixels for recon, latent dims for KL), then mean over the
            # batch axis. Mixing jnp.mean for recon with jnp.sum for KL is the
            # single most common bug in VAE code: it leaves the two terms on
            # wildly different scales and the KL silently dominates.
            obs_variance = 1.0  # fixed Gaussian likelihood; declare/tune explicitly
            squared_error = jnp.mean(
                jnp.sum((x_recon - batch) ** 2, axis=tuple(range(1, batch.ndim)))
            )
            recon_loss = squared_error / (2.0 * obs_variance)
            kl_per_sample = -0.5 * jnp.sum(
                1 + log_var - mu ** 2 - jnp.exp(log_var), axis=-1
            )
            kl_loss = jnp.mean(kl_per_sample)

            return recon_loss + kl_loss

        # Gradient update (NNX 0.11.0+ API)
        loss, grads = nnx.value_and_grad(loss_fn)(vae)
        optimizer.update(vae, grads)

    Key Training Metrics to Monitor¤

    No one scalar is the primary metric for every VAE use. Monitor at least three layers:

    • Optimization. Plot the stochastic training objective, gradient norms, learning-rate schedule, and non-finite updates. A minibatch curve need not decrease monotonically; Monte Carlo noise, minibatch variation, and deliberate schedules can all move it upward temporarily.
    • Probabilistic fit. Report a held-out ELBO with the declared observation likelihood, reduction, preprocessing, and number of latent samples. When density quality matters, add a held-out importance bound or another declared likelihood estimator. A training ELBO is neither exact test likelihood nor evidence of calibrated posterior predictions.
    • Rate and representation. Report total and groupwise KL, active units, aggregate-prior discrepancy, and task-specific probes or interventions. No raw range such as 5–20 nats transfers across latent dimensions, likelihoods, datasets, or objective weights.
    • Generated and reconstructed observations. Evaluate prior samples, posterior reconstructions, conditional samples, and coverage separately. A falling reconstruction NLL need not improve prior generation, and an image similarity score need not improve density estimation.

    “All near zero” is evidence that the approximate posterior carries little information relative to the prior under the chosen parameterization. It is not automatically a problem: if the observation is predictable without \(z\), the population optimum may legitimately ignore it. Diagnose collapse relative to the modeling goal using reconstructions, prior samples, conditional samples, active units, mutual-information estimates with stated estimators, and interventions on decoder capacity.

    What ELBO Optimization Does Not Guarantee¤

    For the separate Fisher geometries of the generative joint, observed marginal, and amortized posterior—and for the pathwise, score-function, and implicit estimators used to train them—see Gradients and Geometry in Generative Modeling.

    For a fixed generative parameter \(\theta\), the evidence identity gives

    \[ \begin{aligned} &\max_{q\in\mathcal Q}\mathcal L(\theta,q;x)\\ &\quad=\log p_\theta(x)\\ &\qquad-\min_{q\in\mathcal Q}\\[-2pt] &\qquad D_{\mathrm{KL}}\!\bigl( q(z\mid x)\|p_\theta(z\mid x) \bigr). \end{aligned} \]

    If the exact posterior belongs to \(\mathcal Q\) and the variational optimization finds it, the gap is zero. Otherwise, even a global maximizer of the restricted ELBO can prefer a different \(\theta\) from a maximum-likelihood solution because model fit and posterior approximability are optimized together. With a neural decoder, amortized encoder, minibatches, and Monte Carlo gradients, the joint problem is also non-convex and stochastic. Convergence of a training curve does not guarantee a local maximum of the ELBO, much less a local maximum-likelihood estimate.

    This boundary is not an objection to variational learning. It identifies what must be measured: held-out model fit, the approximation gap to a stronger per-example family, the amortization gap between shared and locally refined inference, and finite optimization or estimator error. The joint-distribution view in Chapter 1 and the approximate-inference chapter provide the corresponding diagnostic constructions.

    Worked reduction audit¤

    Take a batch of two \(2\times2\) scalar images and a two-dimensional latent. Suppose per-example summed Gaussian reconstruction NLLs (dropping the same fixed constant) are \((4,8)\) and KLs are \((1,3)\). The batch-mean negative ELBO is

    \[ \frac{(4+1)+(8+3)}{2}=8. \]

    If reconstruction error were instead averaged over all eight pixels, it would become \(12/8=1.5\), while leaving KL as a batch mean gives \(2\), producing 3.5. Neither reduction is inherently illegal, but they encode different effective weights. A paper or implementation that calls both simply “reconstruction + KL” is underspecified. State whether each term is summed or averaged over examples, pixels/features, time, and latent groups.

    Unit tests for a VAE loss¤

    Before a large training run, test these small invariants:

    • mu = 0 and log_var = 0 must give zero KL to a standard normal prior.
    • Increasing one mean coordinate from 0 to 1 must add \(1/2\) nat to that example's KL.
    • Two independent Monte Carlo samples should differ when RNG keys differ and repeat when the key is replayed intentionally.
    • Duplicating every example in a batch should leave a batch-mean loss unchanged.
    • Reshaping observation axes without changing values should leave a summed-per-example reconstruction term unchanged.
    • Gradients and parameters should be finite after the optimizer update; finite loss alone is insufficient.

    Independent practice and assessment map: training¤

    Work from unreduced per-example quantities. Every reported scalar must name its batch, observation, time, and latent reductions and the RNG draw that produced its Monte Carlo term.

    P1 — Validate a stochastic negative-ELBO training step¤

    A batch has two examples with summed reconstruction NLLs \((6,10)\). Their diagonal posteriors have \((\mu,\sigma^2)=((1,0),(1,4))\) and \(((0,-1),(1,1))\) against a standard normal prior.

    1. P1.1 — Predict before computing. Predict which reported quantities change if each observation coordinate is duplicated while reconstruction remains a mean over coordinates and KL remains a sum over latent dimensions. Then predict what should happen to a correctly defined batch-mean loss if the two examples are duplicated as records.
    2. P1.2 — Finite reduction audit. Calculate each posterior KL, each negative ELBO, and the batch mean. Recompute the scalar when reconstruction is averaged over four observation coordinates but KL is unchanged. State why both calculations can be executable yet represent different rate–distortion weights.
    3. P1.3 — Implementation and RNG test. Specify shapes for mu, log_var, noise, reconstruction NLL, KL, and per-example loss. Give tests for the zero-KL case, replayed versus split keys, duplicated batches, finite gradients after update, and accidental reduction over the batch before latent dimensions.
    4. P1.4 — Gap diagnosis. A training curve plateaus with near-zero rate and poor held-out likelihood. Design controls that separately probe decoder/model misspecification, a restricted posterior family, amortization error, Monte Carlo estimator variance, and optimization failure. State why the converged scalar cannot establish maximum likelihood.
    5. P1.5 — Transfer. Adapt the loss to padded variable-length sequences. Define the observation mask, per-token likelihood, sequence and corpus denominators, RNG ownership, and a test showing that adding padding cannot improve the reported bound or perplexity.

    Chapter 2 synthesis¤

    The negative ELBO is simple to write and easy to change accidentally. Observation scale, reduction axes, Monte Carlo samples, and RNG splitting all alter the estimator or effective objective. Diagnostics must identify whether the model ignores latents, the inference family misses the posterior, or the decoder likelihood fits the data poorly; one scalar ELBO cannot separate those causes. A restricted, amortized, stochastically optimized ELBO is a principled objective, not a guarantee that training reached a maximum-likelihood solution.

    Retrieval practice: VAE training
    1. What KL results from \(q(z\mid x)=\mathcal N(0,I)\) and \(p(z)=\mathcal N(0,I)\)?
    2. Why can mean-pixel reconstruction plus summed-latent KL change with image resolution?
    3. Is a zero KL dimension necessarily a bug?
    4. Why should the sampling key be split outside the differentiated loss closure each step?
    5. Can a global optimum of a restricted ELBO differ from the maximum-likelihood parameter?

    Solutions. (1) Zero. (2) Averaging reconstruction removes its dimensional scaling while summed KL retains latent scaling, changing their relative weight as resolution changes. (3) No; it can be unused at the optimum, though widespread collapse may violate the representation goal. (4) It makes stochastic draws explicit and prevents accidental reuse of identical noise across updates while keeping differentiation over model parameters clear. (5) Yes. When the exact posterior is outside the variational family, the optimized bound subtracts a parameter-dependent approximation gap, so its preferred \(\theta\) need not maximize marginal likelihood.

    Bridge to Chapter 3. When the basic objective fails to yield the desired rate, representation, or reconstruction behavior, variants modify priors, posteriors, bottlenecks, and objective weights. Each modification must be tied to the failure it is meant to address.
